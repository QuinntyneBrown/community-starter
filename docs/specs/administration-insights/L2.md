# ADMN — Administration and insights behavior

## L2-ADMN-001: Change versioned Community settings

**Traces to:** L1-ADMN-001

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Authorized administrators change bounded Community settings against a current version with schema,
policy, dependency, and production-safety validation.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given an authorized administrator and valid current settings version, when an allowed change is submitted, then the new version and Audit Event commit atomically.
2. Given invalid branding, visibility, join, retention, provider-dependent, or internally inconsistent configuration, when submitted, then field-safe validation is returned and active settings remain unchanged.
3. Given a stale version or concurrent update, when submitted, then a conflict returns current state and neither change is silently overwritten.
4. Given a setting would weaken a controlling platform security, privacy, accessibility, or safety baseline, when requested, then the server rejects it regardless of displayed controls.

### States and accessibility

Show loading, draft changes, validation, unsaved, saving, saved, conflict, and blocked-by-policy states;
group controls semantically, preserve valid input, and announce outcomes.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: settings schema, policy floor, dependency, and version rules.
- Integration/contract: read/update, invalid, stale, Audit Event, and cross-Community isolation.
- Component: settings form, error summary, diff, conflict, and saved states.
- Journey/live: race settings changes and prove only one reviewed version becomes active.
- Manual/rendered: keyboard, screen reader, zoom/reflow, long labels, and high-contrast review.

## L2-ADMN-002: Delegate Roles without privilege escalation

**Traces to:** L1-ADMN-001

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Role assignment, removal, and definition changes are server-scoped to the current Community and
cannot grant authority the actor lacks or leave the Community without an eligible owner.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given an authorized actor and eligible Membership, when an allowed Role change is requested, then effective Permissions, actor, target, reason, and Audit Event commit together.
2. Given the actor attempts to grant a Permission or target hierarchy they cannot administer, when requested through UI, API, bulk, or import, then it is denied and Role state remains unchanged.
3. Given removal, Membership suspension, Account deactivation, or deletion would leave no eligible owner, when requested, then a conflict identifies safe remediation and no continuity state changes.
4. Given Role or Membership state changes, when later requests or realtime connections occur, then current authority is reevaluated immediately rather than using stale client claims.

### States and accessibility

Show effective Permission summary, impact, stronger-verification, confirmation, conflict, continuity
remediation, and success states with explicit text rather than color-only hierarchy.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: delegation ceiling, hierarchy, continuity, and effective-Permission rules.
- Integration/contract: assign/remove, bulk bypass, last-owner, audit, and live revocation.
- Component: Role matrix, impact preview, confirmation, and remediation states.
- Journey/live: delegate then revoke a Role and prove no cross-Community or stale-session authority.
- Manual/rendered: keyboard matrix, screen-reader headers, destructive copy, and focus review.

## L2-ADMN-003: Preview and execute high-impact operations

**Traces to:** L1-ADMN-001

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Archive, close, transfer, suspend, bulk membership, bulk content, and other high-impact operations
require current authority, bounded scope, explicit impact, and durable per-target results.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given an authorized high-impact request, when previewed, then estimated targets, irreversible effects, Deliveries, retention, Entitlement, and recovery consequences are shown from current state.
2. Given stronger verification, approval, ownership continuity, reason, or current version is required and missing, when execution is requested, then no target changes.
3. Given a valid confirmed operation executes, when it commits, then idempotent operation identity, actor, scope, reason, and per-target outcomes are auditable under the declared atomicity policy.
4. Given targets change after preview or mixed eligibility exists, when execution begins, then stale or ineligible targets fail explicitly and no silent partial success is reported.
5. Given a suspension, removal, restriction, or bulk content/Membership change has a punitive or policy-enforcement purpose, when execution is attempted, then the administration path requires an eligible Moderation Case and typed Moderation Action per target or rejects the operation without changes.

### States and accessibility

Show calculating, preview, warnings, stronger verification, confirmation, running, per-item outcome,
partial failure, and recovery states; long operations expose progress text and safe cancellation rules.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: operation scope, preview version, approval, atomicity, and idempotency rules.
- Integration/contract: preview/execute, stale target, mixed outcome, Audit Event, and retry behavior.
- Component: impact preview, confirmation, progress, results, and recovery states.
- Journey/live: execute a mixed bulk operation and reconcile every target and downstream effect.
- Manual/rendered: keyboard bulk flow, screen-reader progress, destructive clarity, and zoom review.

## L2-ADMN-004: Separate operator responsibilities

**Traces to:** L1-ADMN-002

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Platform configuration, security, safety, support, billing, audit, and Community administration use
separate least-privilege authorities rather than one unrestricted administrator role.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given an operator Session, when a protected administrative action is requested, then current operator role, purpose, target scope, stronger verification, and action policy are evaluated server-side.
2. Given an operator lacks the specific responsibility, when direct, bulk, API, export, or background action is attempted, then it is denied and no resource details or state changes occur.
3. Given an operator role is granted or revoked, when the transition commits, then approval, actor, scope, expiry if any, and Audit Event are recorded and active Sessions reevaluate authority.
4. Given a deployment has no provisioned operator, when it starts, then no default password, seeded privileged Account, or client-configured bypass creates one.

### States and accessibility

Operator surfaces identify active responsibility and scope, show safe forbidden/remediation states,
and keep security, safety, billing, and support actions visually and semantically distinct.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: responsibility matrix, grant/revoke, expiry, and bootstrap rules.
- Integration/contract: each role/action pair, alternate-path bypass, and live revocation.
- Component: scope indicator, distinct work areas, and remediation states.
- Journey/live: provision, use, and revoke each operator responsibility with Session reevaluation.
- Manual/rendered: role clarity, keyboard navigation, screen reader, and privilege-confusion review.

## L2-ADMN-005: Grant purpose-bound support access

**Traces to:** L1-ADMN-002

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Support access is tied to a valid case, declared purpose, minimum resource scope, expiry, approval,
and current operator identity; ordinary lookup does not expose private content or secrets.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given an authorized Support Operator and valid support case, when scoped access is requested, then purpose, resources, allowed actions, approver where required, and automatic expiry are recorded.
2. Given no valid case, expired scope, unrelated Community, or excessive requested data, when access is attempted, then it is denied and private details are not disclosed.
3. Given support lookup is permitted, when results are returned, then only minimum Account, Community, Delivery, or operation metadata needed for the purpose is shown and sensitive fields are redacted.
4. Given support access expires or is revoked, when another read or action occurs, then it fails immediately and open views cannot continue using stale authorization.

### States and accessibility

Show case context, requested scope, approval, active-until, expiring, expired, redacted, and denied
states; an always-visible scope indicator is programmatically associated with the operator view.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: case purpose, minimum scope, approval, redaction, and expiry rules.
- Integration/contract: request/grant, unrelated target, read/action, audit, and live expiry behavior.
- Component: scope request, approval, active banner, redaction, and expired state.
- Journey/live: grant narrow access, inspect allowed metadata, expire it, and prove immediate denial.
- Manual/rendered: privacy inspection, keyboard, screen reader, persistent scope, and zoom review.

## L2-ADMN-006: Constrain support impersonation

**Traces to:** L1-ADMN-002

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

If impersonation is enabled, it is a time-bounded support aid with explicit authorization,
continuous indication, per-request scope enforcement, dual operator/subject attribution, and a
versioned closed read allow-list. The baseline permits only Account lifecycle/status, non-secret
client configuration, Profile display, Community/Space Membership/Role/effective-Permission
metadata, feature/Entitlement availability, redacted Delivery diagnostics, and requester-safe Support
Case state. It excludes user-authored bodies, private destinations, and identity-bearing activity.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given an approved Support Case, stronger-verified operator, eligible Account, and allowed purpose, when impersonation starts, then a short-lived session records operator, subject, exact resource/field scope, read-allow-list version, reason, approval, and expiry; a later allow-list expansion cannot enlarge that Session.
2. Given impersonation is active, when a request would read Message/Conversation bodies or Attachments, drafts/restricted content, Event access links/precise venue/roster, Notification bodies/destinations, billing documents/instruments, exports, Reports/case evidence, secrets/tokens, or third-party data outside the exact allow-list, or would change any credentials, factors, billing, privacy, Role/Permission, moderation, relationship, content, Message, Event/RSVP, preference, or integration state, then the server denies it regardless of UI visibility.
3. Given the operator ends, expires, revokes, or loses approval, when the next request occurs, then subject context terminates and cannot be restored from a stale token.
4. Given impersonation is disabled, when a known route or protocol is called, then no alternate header, query, or client state can activate it.
5. Given an allowed impersonated read or support annotation occurs, when it completes or is denied, then the Audit Event records operator Account, subject Account, Support Case, purpose, scope, action, outcome, and correlation without attributing the action solely to the subject.

### States and accessibility

Use a persistent, high-contrast, screen-reader-announced impersonation banner with subject, purpose,
expiry, and exit action; never style it so it can be mistaken for an ordinary Account session.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: eligibility, expiry, prohibited-action, and disabled-profile rules.
- Integration/contract: start/end, stale token, every prohibited action, and alternate-path bypass.
- Component: persistent banner, countdown/time, exit, and prohibited-action explanation.
- Journey/live: impersonate, probe sensitive paths, expire the session, and prove operator separation.
- Manual/rendered: unmistakable context, keyboard exit, screen reader, zoom, and high-contrast review.

## L2-ADMN-007: Control break-glass access

**Traces to:** L1-ADMN-002

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Emergency access is a separately protected, narrowly scoped, rapidly expiring path with explicit
justification, immediate notification, complete audit, and mandatory post-use review.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given a documented emergency and eligible stronger-verified responder, when break-glass access is requested, then an independent approver or pre-reviewed quorum control, exact scope, reason, maximum expiry, and immediate alert are established before use; an approver cannot be the requester, access recipient, target subject, personally interested beneficiary, or a conflicted owner of the affected Case or resource.
2. Given normal access is sufficient, prerequisites are missing, requested scope exceeds policy, or the request targets the responder's or approver's own Account, private data, dispute, benefit, or Case without the accepted separately reviewed emergency quorum, when requested, then access is denied and the attempt and declared conflict are audited.
3. Given break-glass access is active, when actions occur, then every read, export, mutation, and denial is correlated to the grant; credential extraction, Audit Event alteration, reporter exposure without safety purpose, disabling backup/evidence, and self-extension remain non-bypassable.
4. Given the grant ends, when the configured post-use deadline elapses, then access is already revoked and an independent reviewer must have recorded evidence, actions, impact, and follow-up or an owned overdue alert fires without editing original Audit Events.

### States and accessibility

Emergency state is persistent and unmistakable, with remaining time, scope, monitoring, and exit;
urgent presentation avoids flashing and remains keyboard and screen-reader usable.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: emergency eligibility, approval, scope, expiry, and review rules.
- Integration/contract: grant/deny, event correlation, auto-revoke, alert, and post-review behavior.
- Component: emergency warning, scope, expiry, exit, and review form.
- Journey/live: activate a test emergency grant, perform bounded work, expire, and complete review.
- Manual/rendered: runbook accuracy, keyboard exit, screen reader, and high-contrast review.

## L2-ADMN-008: Preserve configuration history and rollback

**Traces to:** L1-ADMN-003

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Operational configuration is schema-validated, environment-scoped, versioned, secret-safe, and
changed through staged activation or an explicit new rollback version.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given an authorized operator and valid non-secret configuration, when a change is proposed, then diff, affected environments/capabilities, dependencies, validation, actor, and reason are recorded.
2. Given a value is secret, a production placeholder, malformed, or incompatible, when validation runs, then it is rejected or routed through the secret store without exposing its value in history or logs.
3. Given an approved version activates, when runtime adoption occurs, then active version and health are observable and partial adoption triggers an alert or automatic safe halt.
4. Given rollback is required, when confirmed, then a new audited version restores compatible values; immutable history is not edited and data compatibility is checked first.

### States and accessibility

Show draft, diff, validation, approval, scheduled, activating, active, degraded, and rollback states;
diffs and secrets use semantic text, not color-only indications.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: version, schema, secret classification, activation, and rollback rules.
- Integration/contract: validate, approve, deploy, partial adoption, audit, and rollback behavior.
- Component: diff, validation, approval, health, and rollback states.
- Journey/live: activate an invalid/degraded test change and restore through a new version.
- Manual/rendered: secret redaction, keyboard diff, screen-reader changes, and runbook review.

### Verification

## L2-ADMN-013: Maintain Support Case lifecycle

**Traces to:** L1-ADMN-002

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

A Support Case is the durable purpose boundary for platform support. It has requester or source,
subject, Community/resource scope, categorized purpose, owner, priority, status, bounded notes and
attachments, creation/update/closure times, retention class, linked access grants, and tamper-evident
history. It is distinct from a Moderation Case and cannot authorize safety enforcement.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given an Account request or authorized operator intake with a supported purpose, when accepted, then one Support Case is created with subject, minimum resource scope, owner/queue, priority, retention class, and safe acknowledgement.
2. Given a Case transitions among open, in-progress, waiting-for-requester, resolved, reopened, or closed, when committed, then current actor, reason, time, next owner/action, and Audit Event are recorded without overwriting history.
3. Given a Case closes, expires, changes scope, or loses its valid purpose, when the transition commits, then linked support-access and impersonation grants are revoked immediately and stale Sessions cannot continue.
4. Given notes or Attachments contain private data, when accessed, exported, retained, or deleted, then Support Case purpose, role, redaction, safe rendering, and lifecycle policy apply and ordinary Community administrators cannot read them.
5. Given an assigned owner loses Account eligibility, Support Role, case scope, or conflict independence, when authorization changes or work next runs, then assignment and case-access grants are revoked, the Case returns to an eligible queue under its existing service target, and an owned alert fires if no eligible queue exists.

### States and accessibility

Support tools expose status, priority, requester-safe updates, owner, scope, waiting reason, closure,
reopen, redaction, and access-expired states with accessible timelines and forms.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Test status, ownership, purpose, scope, closure revocation, reopen, and retention rules.
- Integration/contract: Create, assign, annotate, grant access, close/expire, revoke, and enforce separation from moderation.
- Component: Test intake, queue, timeline, notes, requester status, and closed states.
- Journey/live: Resolve a Case using scoped access and prove closure terminates every grant.
- Manual/rendered: Support, privacy, security, safety-separation, and accessibility review.

## L2-ADMN-014: Provision and preserve operator authority

**Traces to:** L1-ADMN-002

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Initial and recovery provisioning of Platform Operator responsibilities uses a reviewed out-of-band
identity and deployment process, never a seeded password. Security, safety, billing, audit, privacy,
and support grants require separation of duties; an operator cannot self-grant or self-approve a
privilege, and configured minimum continuity is preserved without preventing emergency revocation.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given a new production environment has no Platform Operator, when initial provisioning runs, then a designated verified identity receives only the declared bootstrap responsibility through a one-time audited deployment control and no reusable default credential exists.
2. Given an operator requests a high-impact responsibility, when grant approval runs, then a distinct eligible approver or reviewed quorum authorizes exact scope and expiry; self-grant, self-approval, and granting authority the approver cannot delegate are rejected.
3. Given revocation, departure, or expiry would remove the configured last eligible operator for a required responsibility, when requested outside an emergency, then successor acceptance is required before removal and concurrent changes preserve the minimum.
4. Given the last operator is lost, compromised, or must be revoked immediately, when recovery runs, then the documented out-of-band/quorum path revokes unsafe access first, provisions bounded replacement authority, alerts owners, and creates immutable Audit Events and post-use review.

### States and accessibility

Provisioning and grants show bootstrap, requested, independently approved, active, expiring, blocked
by continuity, emergency recovery, revoked, and reviewed states without revealing authentication
secrets.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Test delegation, self-approval, quorum, minimum continuity, expiry, and emergency precedence.
- Integration/contract: Provision empty environment, grant/revoke, race last-operator changes, and execute recovery.
- Component: Test grant, approval, continuity remediation, and recovery states.
- Journey/live: Bootstrap, add a second operator, rotate authority, and recover a compromised last operator.
- Manual/rendered: Security, governance, audit, operations, and accessibility review.

## L2-ADMN-015: Protect Support Case Attachments

**Traces to:** L1-ADMN-002

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

A Support Case Attachment remains quarantined until bounded processing succeeds and is thereafter
available only through the Case's current purpose, participant, access, retention, and audit policy.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given an authorized requester or purpose-bound Support Operator starts an upload for an open Support Case, when an upload intent is issued, then it is bound to that Case, actor, purpose, expected type and size, expiry, and one stable operation identity; it grants no public or cross-Case URL and incomplete bytes are inaccessible.
2. Given bytes complete within accepted limits, when processing runs, then actual signature, media type, size, archive expansion, malware, and active-content policy are validated in quarantine; promotion atomically rechecks the bound Case version, open purpose, actor authority, retention, and hold fence while linking the blob, so only a successful current intent becomes approved and a stale, closed, unsafe, mismatched, expired, or partial intent becomes rejected or cleanup-pending without execution.
3. Given an approved Attachment is listed, previewed, downloaded, exported, or safely rendered, when requested, then current Support Case participant or purpose-bound Role, scope, and status are reauthorized, filenames and content are encoded safely, downloads use bounded non-public authorization, sensitive access is audited, and guessed or ordinary Community-administrator access is denied safely.
4. Given processing, preview, or storage fails or is retried, when recovery runs, then the same Attachment lifecycle resumes idempotently, no duplicate Case entry or orphan authority appears, and a bounded failure offers safe replacement or authorized operator remediation without exposing scanner or storage internals.
5. Given the Case closes, its Attachment retention expires, deletion applies, an upload intent expires, or a valid legal hold changes, when lifecycle work commits, then a monotonically versioned cleanup fence invalidates outstanding intents and promotions, each worker rechecks it before blob or linkage change, and original and derived blobs, staged parts, grants, and metadata are retained, minimized, or erased exactly by policy with no stale resurrection, idempotent cleanup, and an auditable exception where required.

### States and accessibility

Show selecting, uploading, processing, approved, rejected-safe, unavailable, held, removal-pending,
removed, and retry states with progress text, accessible errors, safe filenames, and no automatic media.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: intent scope, limits, processing outcomes, access policy, retention, and cleanup.
- Integration/contract: quarantine, scanner and storage failures, Case-close/promotion race, authorization, download, export, and deletion.
- Component: upload progress, processing, safe preview, rejection, replacement, and unavailable states.
- Journey/live: attach a valid file, reject hostile files, revoke Case access, close, and prove cleanup.
- Manual/rendered: active-content safety, filename encoding, keyboard, screen reader, zoom, and privacy review.
