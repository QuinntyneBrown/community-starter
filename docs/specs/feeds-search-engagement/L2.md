# DISC — Feeds, search, and engagement behavior

## L2-DISC-001: Build the Account Feed

**Traces to:** L1-DISC-001

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

An Account Feed contains eligible Posts and individual published upcoming Event occurrences selected from current
Memberships, Follows, and chosen Tags, then filtered by Community, Space, audience, Permission,
Block, Mute, Moderation Action, Event lifecycle, and retention state.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given an eligible Account with source relationships, when its Feed is requested, then every Post or Event occurrence comes from an authorized source, identifies its item type and stable series-plus-occurrence identity where applicable, and includes enough context to explain why it appears; a recurring series contributes at most one item per eligible occurrence, never a duplicate series summary for that occurrence.
2. Given a Post, Event series or occurrence, Community, Space, Membership, Follow, Block, Mute, audience, or Moderation Action state makes an item ineligible, when the Feed response is built, then the item is omitted even if a cache still contains it.
3. Given the Account has no eligible Post or Event occurrence, when the Feed is requested, then an honest empty state and permitted next actions are returned without fabricated activity.
4. Given a client supplies Membership, Follow, Permission, or ranking claims, when the Feed is requested, then the server ignores them as authority and derives current eligibility.

### States and accessibility

Show loading, populated, empty, refreshing, access-changed, end, and error states; use a Feed
landmark, logical headings, meaningful Post or Event-occurrence actions, and no forced focus.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Post/Event-occurrence source inclusion, series/occurrence deduplication, lifecycle, and filter precedence.
- Integration/contract: mixed items, recurring-occurrence deduplication, Community, Follow, Block, Mute, empty, and forged claims.
- Component: Feed loading, items, context reasons, empty, refresh, and error states.
- Journey/live: alter Membership, Follow, Block, and Mute while the Account Feed is open.
- Manual/rendered: keyboard Feed, screen-reader landmarks, reflow, and live-update noise review.

## L2-DISC-002: Build a Community Feed

**Traces to:** L1-DISC-001

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

A Community Feed contains only eligible Posts and published upcoming Event occurrences owned by that Community
and applies the viewer's current Membership, relationship safety, audience, lifecycle, content, and
visibility rules on every response.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given a viewer authorized for an active Community, when its Feed is requested, then only eligible Posts and Event occurrences whose authoritative Community matches the route are returned with stable type-specific and series-plus-occurrence identity.
2. Given the viewer lacks required Membership or the Community is private or archived, when its Feed is requested, then the server returns the safe unavailable or restricted contract and no Post or Event-occurrence data.
3. Given a Post, Event, Tag, cursor, occurrence, or filter identifier originates in another Community, when used against the Feed, then it grants no access and cannot introduce cross-Community results.
4. Given Block, Mute, audience, Event series/occurrence lifecycle, or Moderation Action filtering removes eligible-looking rows, when the Feed is returned, then page filling remains bounded and never substitutes unauthorized Posts or Event occurrences.

### States and accessibility

Show Community identity, loading, populated, empty, restricted/archived, filtered, end, and error;
maintain heading hierarchy and provide keyboard-safe recovery to permitted Community navigation.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: same-Community Post/occurrence identity, series deduplication, and viewer eligibility rules.
- Integration/contract: recurring occurrences, member/non-member, archived/private, cross-Community cursor/filter, and safety cases.
- Component: Community Feed, empty, archived/restricted, paging, and error states.
- Journey/live: compare the same route for member, non-member, blocked, and revoked contexts.
- Manual/rendered: privacy copy, keyboard navigation, screen-reader headings, and reflow review.

## L2-DISC-003: Page a Feed consistently

**Traces to:** L1-DISC-001

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Feed cursors are opaque, bounded to Account and Feed scope, and produce deterministic mixed Post and
Event-occurrence paging without duplicates under publication, occurrence generation, reschedule,
cancellation, completion, removal, and access changes.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given a valid first-page request, when the Feed has more eligible Posts or Event occurrences, then results include an opaque continuation cursor tied to the viewer, Feed type, scope, filters, ordering policy, and mixed-item snapshot boundary.
2. Given the cursor is valid and unexpired, when the next page is requested, then an already returned type/resource/occurrence identity is not repeated and newly published, generated, or rescheduled items do not reorder the consumed window.
3. Given a cursor is altered, expired, belongs to another Account or Community, or conflicts with filters, when used, then the server rejects it and returns no scoped data.
4. Given Posts or Event occurrences in the next window become inaccessible, cancelled, completed outside policy, removed, or otherwise ineligible, when the page is built, then they are omitted and authorization is never relaxed merely to fill the requested size.

### States and accessibility

Show initial loading, loading more, end, refresh-new-items, expired/restart, and error states; load-more
is keyboard operable, updates preserve reading position, and result counts are not the only cue.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: cursor scope, ordering window, expiry, and duplicate prevention rules.
- Integration/contract: publish/generate/reschedule/cancel/complete/remove between pages, tamper, cross-scope, and filter change.
- Component: load-more, new-items prompt, end, expired, and retry states.
- Journey/live: page while another Account publishes/removes and while access changes.
- Manual/rendered: keyboard load-more, focus/scroll preservation, and announcement review.

## L2-DISC-004: Explain and control ranking

**Traces to:** L1-DISC-001

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Supported Feed orderings and optional personalization use declared signals, expose a concise reason,
and preserve an Account control path without using sensitive inference as access proof.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given a ranked Post or Event occurrence is displayed, when the Account asks why, then a type-appropriate safe reason category such as current Membership, Follow, chosen Tag, or upcoming Community activity is available without revealing another Account's private activity or proprietary security detail.
2. Given the Account selects a supported ordering or disables optional personalization, when the preference commits, then subsequent Feed pages use that choice while all authorization and safety filters remain active.
3. Given the Account clears declared Feed-history signals, when deletion completes, then those optional inputs stop influencing ranking according to retention policy without deleting Posts, Events, RSVPs, Follows, or Memberships.
4. Given ranking service is unavailable, when the Feed is requested, then the platform uses the documented safe fallback or explicit unavailable state and never returns unfiltered data.

### States and accessibility

Show ordering selection, reason disclosure, saving, clear-history confirmation, fallback, and error;
controls have explicit labels/state, reasons are textual, and no auto-reordering steals focus.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: ordering, optional-signal, safety-filter, clearing, and fallback rules.
- Integration/contract: preference, explanations, history clear, service failure, and privacy cases.
- Component: ordering control, reason disclosure, confirmation, fallback, and save states.
- Journey/live: switch ordering, disable personalization, and fail ranking while preserving access filters.
- Manual/rendered: keyboard controls, screen-reader state, plain-language, and motion review.

## L2-DISC-005: Search eligible records

**Traces to:** L1-DISC-002

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Search returns bounded Search results for supported Posts, Events, Communities, Profiles, and Tags
only after current authoritative Community, Space, Membership, audience, visibility, Block,
Moderation Action, Event lifecycle, and retention filtering.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given a valid query, when Search runs, then every Search result identifies its supported type and contains only fields the Account may currently view.
2. Given matching records are private, cross-Community, draft, removed, blocked, hidden, or otherwise ineligible, when Search runs, then they and their restricted snippets/counts are omitted.
3. Given an index contains stale or over-broad data, when a candidate is hydrated or returned, then authoritative filtering prevents disclosure before asynchronous index repair.
4. Given a query is empty, too short/long, malformed, or exceeds supported complexity, when submitted, then stable validation is returned and no broad unbounded Search executes.

### States and accessibility

Show query help, searching, grouped/mixed results, no results, corrected/refined suggestion where
supported, validation, and error; use a search landmark, result headings, and announced result summary.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: query bounds, result-type, visibility, and authoritative-filter rules.
- Integration/contract: all types, cross-Community/private/blocked/stale-index, malformed, and sparse cases.
- Component: search form, loading, grouped results, empty, validation, and error states.
- Journey/live: index an eligible item, restrict it, then prove direct and Search access both deny.
- Manual/rendered: keyboard search/results, screen-reader summary, reflow, and snippet semantics.

## L2-DISC-006: Filter and page Search results

**Traces to:** L1-DISC-002

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Search filters, sorting, facets, and cursors are validated, scope-bound, and calculated only from the
result population the Account is permitted to know about.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given supported type, Community, Tag, author or organizer, date or occurrence, or ordering filters, when Search runs, then they are normalized and applied consistently to results and continuation state.
2. Given a requested Community, Tag, Profile, or filter value is inaccessible, when Search runs, then it does not disclose hidden matches or facet counts and cannot broaden scope.
3. Given a valid continuation cursor, when the next page is requested with the same query and filters, then results continue deterministically without duplicates under the declared freshness policy.
4. Given a cursor is altered, expired, cross-Account/cross-Community, or paired with changed query/filter intent, when used, then the server rejects it and returns no result data.

### States and accessibility

Show applied filters as removable text, updating, results, no matching filters, more/end, expired, and
error states; controls use labels/fieldsets, updates preserve focus, and counts are announced sparingly.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: filter normalization, facet privacy, cursor scope, and ordering rules.
- Integration/contract: combined filters, inaccessible facets, paging, tamper, expiry, and query change.
- Component: filter controls/chips, results update, empty, load-more, and expired states.
- Journey/live: filter and page across two Communities, then revoke access between pages.
- Manual/rendered: keyboard filters, screen-reader groups, focus stability, and narrow-screen review.

## L2-DISC-007: Remove stale Search results

**Traces to:** L1-DISC-002

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Published, edited, rescheduled, cancelled, completed, restricted, removed, and restored records drive
retryable Search projection changes, while result-time authorization remains the final disclosure
boundary.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given an eligible record commits publication or an edit, when projection succeeds, then searchable fields and version correspond to the committed safe representation.
2. Given a record becomes private, removed, blocked, de-identified, or cross-scope inaccessible, when Search is requested before cleanup finishes, then authoritative filtering suppresses the stale result.
3. Given projection delivery fails after content commits, when retry occurs, then projection converges idempotently without duplicate Search results or rolling back source content.
4. Given a removed record is lawfully restored, when reindexing completes, then it returns only to Accounts authorized under its current state and not its former visibility.

### States and accessibility

When a selected stale Search result disappears, show a generic unavailable state and safe return to
results; refresh changes do not steal focus or announce each removed item.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: projection version, removal, restore, and idempotent convergence rules.
- Integration/contract: publish/edit/restrict/remove/restore, delayed projection, and retry cases.
- Component: stale selection, unavailable, return, and refreshed-results states.
- Journey/live: delay indexing through visibility changes and probe both list and stale direct selection.
- Manual/rendered: privacy copy, keyboard return, focus preservation, and announcement-noise review.

## L2-DISC-008: Add and remove a Reaction

**Traces to:** L1-DISC-003

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

An eligible Account can add, change where supported, or remove its bounded Reaction on accessible
Post or Comment content in the same Community, exactly once per declared Reaction policy.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given accessible eligible content, active Membership where required, an active Space when scoped, and a supported Reaction, when the Account reacts, then its current Reaction state is committed once and the updated permitted summary is returned.
2. Given the same Reaction action is retried, when it already committed, then current state is returned and counts do not increment again.
3. Given a change from one mutually exclusive Reaction to another is supported, when it commits, then old and new state/counts change atomically.
4. Given content is inaccessible, removed, in an archive-pending or archived Space, locked by policy, authored by the other Account in a blocked pair, cross-Community, or the Account lacks Permission, when adding or changing a Reaction is attempted, then the server rejects it and preserves Reaction and count state; removing the Account's own existing Reaction remains allowed under the Space archive policy.

### States and accessibility

Show available choices, selected, submitting, changed, removed, unavailable, and conflict states;
buttons expose names and pressed state, work by keyboard, and do not rely on emoji alone.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: supported set, uniqueness/exclusivity, eligibility, and count rules.
- Integration/contract: add/replay/change/remove, inaccessible, locked, and cross-Community cases.
- Component: Reaction group, pressed/busy/error state, and accessible count summary.
- Journey/live: react from two Accounts, change/remove, and restrict content during interaction.
- Manual/rendered: keyboard group, screen-reader pressed/name, non-color state, and reflow review.

## L2-DISC-009: Save and remove a Bookmark

**Traces to:** L1-DISC-003

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

A Bookmark is private to its owning Account, references accessible content, is unique per target, and
never grants continued access after content or Community authorization changes.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given an Account may access a supported Post or Comment, when Bookmark is requested, then one private Bookmark is saved for that Account and target.
2. Given the Bookmark already exists or removal is retried after absence, when the action is repeated, then the current state is returned without duplicates or negative counts.
3. Given another Account lists or guesses Bookmark identifiers, when access is evaluated, then no Bookmark ownership, target, count, or existence is disclosed.
4. Given bookmarked content becomes inaccessible, removed, or cross-Community unauthorized, when Bookmarks are listed or opened, then content is withheld and the Bookmark follows the declared stale-reference cleanup policy without granting access.

### States and accessibility

Show save/saved/remove states, private Bookmark list loading/empty/paged, stale unavailable item, and
error; buttons expose saved state and list updates preserve keyboard focus.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: ownership, uniqueness, idempotent removal, privacy, and no-access-grant rules.
- Integration/contract: save/replay/remove, cross-Account probes, list paging, and stale target cases.
- Component: Bookmark action and private list loading/empty/stale/error states.
- Journey/live: Bookmark, revoke Membership, and prove list/direct target cannot bypass access.
- Manual/rendered: keyboard, screen-reader saved state, private-copy, and list reflow review.

## L2-DISC-010: Present engagement state

**Traces to:** L1-DISC-003

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Reaction summaries and the current Account's Bookmark/Reaction state are derived from committed,
visible records and follow explicit disclosure, privacy-threshold, and moderation policy.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given accessible content, when engagement state is returned, then the Account's own Reaction and Bookmark state matches committed records and declared public Reaction totals exclude ineligible records.
2. Given a public Reaction total is visible but participant identity is restricted, when details are requested, then only the permitted aggregate is returned and no hidden Account list can be inferred.
3. Given content or a reacting Account is removed, in a blocked pair with the viewer, de-identified, or moderated, when summaries are rebuilt, then per-viewer counts and participant visibility exclude ineligible records under the Block matrix without exposing private status.
4. Given a delayed projection disagrees with the mutation result, when the client reconciles, then authoritative current state eventually replaces provisional state without double-applying the action.

### States and accessibility

Show provisional, committed, delayed/reconciling, aggregate-only, unavailable, and error states;
summaries have accessible text, compact visuals are not color/emoji-only, and updates are not noisy.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: committed-state, aggregate disclosure, privacy threshold, and removal rules.
- Integration/contract: own state, totals/details, blocked/removed/moderated, and delayed projection.
- Component: summaries, own states, provisional/reconcile, aggregate-only, and errors.
- Journey/live: react/bookmark with projection delay and change participant/content visibility.
- Manual/rendered: screen-reader summary, keyboard actions, non-color indicators, and live-update review.

## L2-DISC-011: Reconcile committed changes

**Traces to:** L1-DISC-004

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Committed content, Event, and relationship changes propagate idempotently to Feed, Search, Reaction,
and Bookmark projections; clients reconcile realtime hints with authoritative reads after gaps or
conflict.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given an eligible committed change, when projection and realtime delivery succeed, then events identify stable Community/resource versions and affected views converge to that committed state.
2. Given delivery is duplicated or reordered, when consumers process it, then final Feed, Search result, Reaction, and Bookmark state remains idempotent and version-consistent.
3. Given delivery is missed, disconnected, or fails after commit, when retry/reconnect occurs, then clients and projections re-read from an authoritative checkpoint rather than treating the event stream as truth.
4. Given authorization changes before an event is delivered or reconciled, when the recipient is evaluated, then restricted payloads are withheld and current access wins.
5. Given an event has invalid version, Community scope, or resource identity, when a consumer evaluates it, then the event is rejected and existing projection state is preserved.

### States and accessibility

Show connected where useful, reconnecting, stale/refresh available, reconciling, conflict, and current;
preserve reading position and announce only meaningful Account-initiated outcomes or access changes.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: event identity, version ordering, deduplication, and authorization-before-delivery.
- Integration/contract: duplicate/out-of-order/missed events, projection retry, and revoked access.
- Component: reconnect, stale prompt, reconcile, conflict, and current states.
- Journey/live: inject disconnect, duplicate, reorder, and access revocation across all projections.
- Manual/rendered: focus/scroll stability, announcement noise, offline recovery, and reduced motion.

## L2-DISC-012: Bound abuse and degradation

**Traces to:** L1-DISC-004

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Feed, Search, Reaction, and Bookmark operations enforce query, pagination, frequency, and workload
bounds by Account and broader risk context, with privacy-safe failure and deliberate degradation.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given an Account or origin exceeds a configured operation limit, when another request arrives, then the server returns a stable retry contract and performs no duplicate engagement mutation.
2. Given a query, page size, cursor chain, or filter combination exceeds supported cost, when requested, then it is bounded or rejected without executing an unbounded workload or revealing hidden totals.
3. Given Search, ranking, realtime, or projection dependencies are degraded, when a request is served, then the documented safe fallback or explicit unavailable state is used and authorization filtering is never bypassed.
4. Given limits apply across Communities, when usage is calculated, then one Community cannot consume or infer another Community's private quota details beyond the disclosed policy.

### States and accessibility

Show throttled with textual retry guidance, bounded-query validation, fallback order, offline/reconnect,
and unavailable states; avoid motion-only countdowns and provide keyboard-accessible retry.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: operation limits, cost bounds, fallback, and Community-isolated accounting rules.
- Integration/contract: burst/replay, deep paging, expensive filters, dependency failure, and Community load.
- Component: throttled, validation, fallback, retry, offline, and unavailable states.
- Journey/live: load-test bounded paths and inject each dependency failure while probing authorization.
- Manual/rendered: keyboard retry, screen-reader timing, reduced-motion, and plain-language review.
