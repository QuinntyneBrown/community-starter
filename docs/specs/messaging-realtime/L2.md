# MESS — Messaging and realtime behavior

## L2-MESS-001: Start an authorized Conversation

**Traces to:** L1-MESS-001

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Every Conversation has one immutable Community and optional immutable same-Community Space scope.
The server creates it only when every initial participant is eligible under current Account,
Membership, Space, rules, Moderation Action, Block, and recipient-contact policy.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given two eligible Accounts with active Membership and accepted applicable rules request initial direct contact in the same selected Community and optional Space, when creation is requested in either participant order, then the one stable Conversation for that unordered pair and exact immutable scope is returned or created and the same transaction applies the recipient's direct-admission policy to create either one pending request or one active consent interval; a shell alone grants no participation, and the same pair in another Community is distinct.
2. Given a participant is unknown, inactive, outside the permitted scope, or not contactable, when creation is requested, then the server returns a scoped safe failure and persists no Conversation.
3. Given the same or different idempotency keys concurrently request the same direct Account pair/scope, when creation commits, then a uniqueness constraint and retry policy produce one Conversation and no parallel direct thread.
4. Given an eligible Account has group-creation Permission in the selected Community and optional Space, when a valid group name and participation policy are submitted, then one group Conversation is created with that Account as its sole initial participant and controller, one current history boundary, and a minimum of one eligible controller; every other Account must enter through invitation and acceptance.
5. Given direct or group creation lacks Permission or rules acceptance, uses an inaccessible or cross-Community Space, attempts to migrate an existing Conversation's Community/Space, violates content or participant bounds, or retries a prior operation, when processed, then it respectively creates or changes no Conversation or returns the original result without a duplicate group or controller assignment.

### States and accessibility

Show selecting, validating, created, ineligible, throttled, and unavailable states; identify invalid
participants without leaking private Accounts, retain valid choices, and announce the result.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: participant eligibility, Conversation/admission uniqueness, and idempotency rules.
- Integration/contract: create with pending/preauthorized admission, reciprocal race, scoped failure, retry, and unauthorized contracts.
- Component: participant picker, validation, busy, and result states.
- Journey/live: start a Conversation and open its committed detail.
- Manual/rendered: keyboard, screen-reader, narrow-screen, and error-summary review.

## L2-MESS-002: Enforce Message request and Block policy

**Traces to:** L1-MESS-001

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Recipient preferences, Message-request state, and Blocks are evaluated on the server for discovery,
Conversation creation, sending, notifications, and realtime delivery.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given otherwise eligible Accounts with no prior terminal direct interval start contact, when Account/Membership, Community/Space, rules, recipient contact preference, Block, Moderation Action, capacity, pair/scope request, and interval state are atomically evaluated, then approval-required policy creates exactly one pending request whose recorded recipient alone may decide, while explicit no-approval policy creates exactly one active interval for both participants at the disclosed current `visibleFrom` boundary and terminalizes a stale pending request without Message or Delivery; concurrent, reciprocal, creation, request, or retry paths produce only that one safe pending-or-active result.
2. Given the intended recipient accepts or declines a pending request, when the transition commits, then the server atomically rechecks request target/version/expiry, sender and recipient Account/Membership, Community/Space access and rules, current contact preference, Block, Moderation Action, capacity, request state, and absence of another active interval; only one eligible acceptance opens one disclosed direct consent interval and Deliveries, while stale, ineligible, expired, duplicate, or replayed decisions grant nothing and preserve a privacy-safe terminal/prior result.
3. Given either Account has an applicable Block, when discovery, request, send, notification, or subscription is attempted, then the action reveals no private status and no Message or Delivery is created.
4. Given a Block or Account/Membership eligibility, Community/Space access, applicable rules, contact-policy, or Moderation state ends eligibility for an active direct Conversation, when access is reevaluated, then the current consent interval receives a fixed terminal boundary, further interaction and queued effects stop, prior history follows current visibility/retention policy, authorized evidence remains available, and later eligibility restoration cannot reactivate that interval.
5. Given the original sender withdraws a current request, the intended recipient declines, the idempotent expiry scheduler reaches its deadline, or those transitions race acceptance, no-approval admission, or retry, when the request version commits, then exactly one ordered outcome wins; a terminal withdrawal/decline/expiry suppresses queued request notices, cannot later open an interval, exposes only privacy-safe status, and prevents a new request or alternate Conversation until the configured cooldown, while a previously committed acceptance remains authoritative.

### States and accessibility

Show request pending with sender withdrawal, accepted, declined/unavailable, withdrawn, expired,
cooldown, blocked-safe, and error states without exposing the recipient's private choice; all
decisions and recovery actions are keyboard operable and expiry is announced without a countdown.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: pair/scope request and active-interval uniqueness, version/expiry, acceptance eligibility, and termination matrix.
- Integration/contract: reciprocal admission, accept/withdraw/decline/expiry races, duplicate acceptance, scope loss, Block, notices, and send enforcement.
- Component: pending/withdraw controls, accept/decline, expired/cooldown, and safe unavailable states.
- Journey/live: race withdrawal/expiry with acceptance, then lose/restore scope and require fresh consent.
- Manual/rendered: privacy copy, focus order, announcements, and narrow-screen review.

## L2-MESS-003: Control participant lifecycle

**Traces to:** L1-MESS-001

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Participant invitations, acceptance, removals, voluntary departure, and role changes follow the
Conversation type, current Permissions, explicit consent, and auditable visibility intervals. For a
group, recipient contact preference governs new admission but does not evict an accepted participant;
pairwise Block behavior remains separate.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given a group Conversation and authorized participant manager, when an eligible Account is invited, then one pending invitation is created and no participation, Message access, unread count, Notification, or Delivery begins until that Account accepts.
2. Given an unauthorized actor or ineligible Account, when participation is changed, then the server rejects the action and participant state remains unchanged.
3. Given a group interval begins through accepted invitation or an existing participant leaves, is removed, or loses Account/Membership eligibility, Community/Space access, applicable rules, or group participation through a Moderation Action, when the current participant transition commits, then admission records the disclosed stable `visibleFrom`, while a terminal transition ends interval and Role atomically at a stable `visibleThrough`, terminalizes pending effects, releases capacity, and denies or reauthorizes later Messages, Attachments, unread/receipt/presence, realtime, Notifications, Deliveries, and queued work; a contact-preference change alone governs later group invitations without ending the accepted interval, and Block uses the pairwise group matrix.
4. Given the change would violate the minimum-participant or ownership rule, when requested, then a conflict response explains remediation and changes no state.
5. Given a former participant later regains Account/Membership, Community/Space, rules, or Moderation eligibility, when group access is requested, then the old interval remains terminal and only a fresh current invitation and acceptance may create a new interval whose disclosed `visibleFrom` does not erase prior boundaries, restore old Roles/unread/Deliveries, or expose excluded gaps unless a separately authorized history grant is explicitly accepted.

### States and accessibility

Show current participants, pending change, confirmation, conflict, and removed/left states; announce
membership changes and provide explicit labels and destructive confirmation.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: participant roles, eligibility-loss boundary, admission-only contact policy, history, re-entry, and continuity.
- Integration/contract: add/remove/leave, contact change, Membership/Space/rules/Action loss/restore, queues, and boundary access.
- Component: participant list, confirmation, conflict, and status announcements.
- Journey/live: add a participant, revoke and restore Membership/Space eligibility, then require fresh consent and preserve gaps.
- Manual/rendered: keyboard, screen-reader, long-name, and destructive-flow review.

4. Given an authorized safety reviewer opens a Report, when Block suppression applies to ordinary participants, then the preserved evidence remains reviewable under the Case policy and is never republished into the group.
5. Given participant join, removal, or re-add intervals exist, when any history is requested, then both the interval's `visibleFrom`/`visibleThrough` bounds and current Block/Action policy must allow the item before it is disclosed.

### States and accessibility

Suppressed content uses no Block-revealing label; gaps, unread counts, participant changes, and
unavailable Messages remain understandable to keyboard and screen-reader users without noisy
per-item announcements.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Test pairwise suppression across content, metadata, counts, intervals, and evidence exceptions.
- Integration/contract: Exercise history, notifications, read/presence, realtime, and re-add boundaries.
- Component: Test group continuity, suppressed gaps, unread state, and safe unavailable rendering.
- Journey/live: Block within an active group, send from all participants, reconnect, unblock, remove, and re-add.
- Manual/rendered: Safety, privacy, messaging comprehension, keyboard, and screen-reader review.

## L2-MESS-016: Close and re-enter Conversations deterministically

**Traces to:** L1-MESS-001

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Group closure is terminal, while the one stable direct Conversation for an Account pair and scope may
hold multiple explicitly accepted consent intervals. Participant authority or one confirmed
Space-archive or Community-retirement dependency plan may close exact scope without reading content;
neither closure, leave, Block removal, nor fresh approval silently restores interaction or history.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given a current controller closes a group, either direct participant ends its interval, Account/Membership eligibility, Community/Space access, rules, contact policy, Block, or Moderation state invalidates a direct interval, a current Community administrator holding `ArchiveSpace` executes the confirmed archive plan for an exact Space-scoped Conversation, or a current verified eligible Owner for a voluntary Community-retirement plan or the correlated independent safety executor for an enforcement plan executes that confirmed plan for an exact listed Conversation, when the exact plan and current Community lifecycle version are rechecked and the transition commits, then send and participant `visibleThrough` boundaries are fixed, pending invitations/requests terminalize, new interaction stops, queued effects reauthorize or suppress, and a lifecycle executor gains no Message/Attachment read or participant authority while trigger, plan, identities, Audit Event, and privacy-safe notice are recorded.
2. Given an authorized former participant reads a closed group or ended direct interval, when history is requested, then only Messages and Attachments within that participant's existing visible-from/through intervals and current Block, retention, deletion, evidence, Community/Space, and privacy policy are returned; closure creates no public access, new unread state, receipt, presence, or notification disclosure.
3. Given Message or Attachment retention, participant deletion, or a Report applies after closure, when lifecycle processing runs, then ordinary data is tombstoned, erased, or retained under the same declared policy, protected evidence remains Case-bound, and closure neither accelerates nor defeats a legal hold or eligible safety path.
4. Given a closed group or a direct interval ended through leave, withdrawal, decline, Block, closure, eligibility, scope, rules, contact-policy, or Moderation loss, when eligibility later permits renewed interaction, then the group remains terminal and requires a distinct later Conversation with fresh invitations, while the direct pair requires a fresh unique Message request and recipient acceptance to create one new interval in its same unique Conversation with a new `visibleFrom`, without automatically restoring excluded gaps, prior Deliveries, receipts, or participation.
5.
