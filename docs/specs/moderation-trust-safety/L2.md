# SAFE — Moderation, trust, and safety behavior

## L2-SAFE-001: Apply versioned policy precedence

**Traces to:** L1-SAFE-001

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Every reportable or enforceable decision identifies the applicable version of platform policy and,
where permitted, Community policy, with explicit precedence and effective dates.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given an Account views or joins a Community, when applicable rules are requested, then current platform and Community policy, effective date, scope, and precedence are available in accessible language.
2. Given policy changes materially affect participation or enforcement, when published, then the version is immutable, future cases reference it, and required acknowledgement follows the declared rule.
3. Given a Community rule conflicts with a controlling platform rule or grants broader enforcement authority, when evaluated, then platform policy prevails and no unauthorized rule is enforced.
4. Given an older case is reviewed, when its basis is displayed, then the policy version effective at the relevant event remains retrievable to authorized reviewers.

### States and accessibility

Show current, upcoming, acknowledgement-required, superseded, and unavailable states; preserve
heading structure, readable change summaries, keyboard access, and printable/reflowable content.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: version, effective-date, acknowledgement, and precedence rules.
- Integration/contract: publish, retrieve current/history, conflict, and case-reference contracts.
- Component: policy view, change summary, acknowledgement, and superseded state.
- Journey/live: publish a Community policy change and prove later Reports retain the correct version.
- Manual/rendered: keyboard, screen reader, zoom/reflow, plain language, and print review.

## L2-SAFE-002: Submit a safe Report

**Traces to:** L1-SAFE-001

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

An eligible person can Report supported content, Profile, Membership, Community, Message, Event, or
conduct with a reason, bounded context, safe acknowledgement, and an immutable evidence snapshot
committed in the same transaction. A server-issued report receipt can preserve the reporting path for
a bounded period after a previously viewed target is hidden, deleted, access-restricted, or Blocked.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given an eligible reporter can currently view a supported target or presents an unexpired server-issued receipt from a previously authorized view, when valid reason and optional context are submitted, then one Report, the exact permitted target/version evidence, provenance, and policy context commit atomically.
2. Given a target is private, removed, hidden, Blocked, or known only through a guessed identifier without a valid receipt, when an ineligible Report is attempted, then no Report or evidence is created and target existence, prior state, or receipt validity is not disclosed.
3. Given the same reporter retries the same operation, when processed, then the original acknowledgement is returned without duplicate Report or evidence.
4. Given a Report is accepted, when the reporter views status, then they see a safe receipt and support/emergency guidance but not internal notes, reviewer identity, evidence, or another person's outcome.

### States and accessibility

Show target context, reason selection, optional details, submitting, acknowledged, duplicate-safe,
throttled, and unavailable states with explicit labels, error association, and focus management.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: supported targets, current/receipt eligibility, receipt expiry, reason bounds, evidence atomicity, and idempotency.
- Integration/contract: submit, prior-view receipt, guessed ID, duplicate, rollback, throttle, and reporter-status contracts.
- Component: report flow, validation, acknowledgement, and unavailable state.
- Journey/live: report each sensitive target type and confirm only reporter-safe status is visible.
- Manual/rendered: keyboard, screen reader, narrow-screen, distress-aware copy, and zoom review.

## L2-SAFE-003: Protect reporters and limit abuse

**Traces to:** L1-SAFE-001

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Reporter identity and contact are disclosed only for an authorized purpose, while rate, duplicate,
and malicious-report controls preserve access to urgent safety reporting.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given a normal reported Account or Community administrator, when case information is shown, then reporter identity, contact, Block state, and private narrative are absent unless an approved policy explicitly requires disclosure.
2. Given related Reports concern the same target and event, when triage runs, then they may be linked without losing distinct reporters, evidence, or acknowledgement.
3. Given reporting volume exceeds configured risk thresholds, when another Report is submitted, then proportional throttling or friction applies without revealing target state or silently discarding urgent categories.
4. Given a reporter Blocks the subject or leaves the Community, when the case continues, then the Report and restricted evidence remain valid and retaliation paths remain closed.

### States and accessibility

Explain confidentiality limits, urgent alternatives, throttling, and acknowledgement without making
guarantees the system cannot keep; never expose reporter details in visual previews.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: identity disclosure, duplicate-linking, throttling, and urgent-category rules.
- Integration/contract: subject/admin views, linked Reports, rate controls, and post-departure case state.
- Component: confidentiality, throttle, urgent guidance, and safe status views.
- Journey/live: link duplicate Reports and prove reporter identity is absent from subject-facing paths.
- Manual/rendered: sensitive-data inspection, keyboard, screen reader, and trauma-informed copy review.

## L2-SAFE-004: Create a case with durable evidence

**Traces to:** L1-SAFE-002

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Accepted Reports create or join a Moderation Case by linking the immutable target/version evidence,
provenance, access classification, and policy basis committed with the Report; case processing never
recaptures a later target version as if it were the reported evidence.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given an accepted Report with committed evidence, when case processing commits, then it creates or links one eligible Moderation Case to that exact evidence, metadata, provenance, and policy reference without recapturing current target content.
2. Given content is edited, deleted, hidden, or its author's Account is deactivated or deleted afterward, when reviewed, then authorized reviewers can access the captured evidence and distinguish it from current state.
3. Given evidence snapshot persistence fails, when the Report transaction terminates, then neither Report nor evidence commits; a later case cannot be represented as evidence-complete from that attempt.
4. Given duplicate or out-of-order processing occurs, when retried, then evidence and Report links are not duplicated or silently replaced.

### States and accessibility

Reviewer views distinguish alleged target, captured version, current state, missing evidence, and
provenance using text and semantic structure; sensitive media is concealed until deliberately shown.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: case-linking, evidence version, provenance, and completeness rules.
- Integration/contract: atomic Report/evidence rollback, case linking, edit/delete-after-report, failure, and replay behavior.
- Component: evidence comparison, missing state, and safe media reveal.
- Journey/live: Report then edit/delete each target and review the preserved version.
- Manual/rendered: sensitive-media safety, keyboard reveal, screen-reader structure, and redaction review.

## L2-SAFE-005: Triage, assign, and escalate cases

**Traces to:** L1-SAFE-002

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Moderation Cases and Appeals use explicit priority, status, ownership, service target, and escalation
transitions without allowing queue races or silent abandonment.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given an untriaged case, when classification runs, then supported risk signals set a reviewable priority and service target without treating automation as final enforcement.
2. Given two authorized Moderators attempt assignment, when requests race, then one current owner or declared shared-assignment state commits and the other receives a conflict.
3. Given a case ages, reaches an urgent category, crosses Community authority, or presents reviewer conflict, when escalation rules apply, then it moves to the eligible platform queue with history intact.
4. Given a case is resolved, duplicated, deferred, or closed, when its status changes, then reason, actor, timestamp, next action, and reopen policy are recorded.
5. Given an assignee loses Account eligibility, required Role, case scope, or reviewer independence, when authorization changes or work next runs, then assignment and evidence access are revoked, the work returns to an eligible queue under its existing service target, and an owned alert fires if no eligible queue exists.

### States and accessibility

Queues show priority meaning, age, service-target status, assignment, escalation, filters, and empty
states; tables and controls support keyboard use and do not rely on color or animation alone.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: priority, assignment concurrency, assignee eligibility, status, escalation, and reopen rules.
- Integration/contract: queue, claim race, authority loss, requeue, escalation, closure, and Audit Event behavior.
- Component: filters, assignment, overdue, conflict, escalation, and empty states.
- Journey/live: race assignment, breach a service target, and escalate with full history.
- Manual/rendered: keyboard table, screen-reader headers, zoom, and high-contrast review.

## L2-SAFE-006: Restrict and retain sensitive evidence

**Traces to:** L1-SAFE-002

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Evidence access is least privilege, purpose-bound, audited, redacted where possible, and reconciled
with retention, deletion, legal-hold, and reviewer-safety policies.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given an authorized reviewer with a current case purpose, when evidence is opened or exported, then only fields allowed for that role and case are returned and sensitive access is audited.
2. Given an unauthorized, conflicted, expired, or cross-Community reviewer, when evidence is requested, then access is denied without disclosing evidence existence or content.
3. Given ordinary retention expires, when lifecycle processing runs, then evidence is erased or minimized unless a valid documented hold applies; the result is auditable.
4. Given a hold is placed, extended, or released, when the transition commits, then scope, authority, reason, expiry/review date, and affected evidence are recorded without claiming legal compliance.

### States and accessibility

Show redacted, concealed-media, access-expired, hold, retention-pending, and unavailable states;
provide keyboard-safe reveals, warnings, and a text alternative for non-visual evidence metadata.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: evidence access, redaction, retention, and hold transition rules.
- Integration/contract: authorized/denied read, export, audit, expiry, deletion, and hold behavior.
- Component: redaction, reveal warning, hold status, and access-expired states.
- Journey/live: retain evidence under a hold, release it, and verify scheduled minimization.
- Manual/rendered: reviewer safety, screen-reader metadata, keyboard reveal, and privacy inspection.

## L2-SAFE-007: Bound moderator authority and conflicts

**Traces to:** L1-SAFE-003

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Community Moderators, platform safety operators, and administrators receive separate action scopes;
current role, target, policy, conflict, and severity determine authority for every decision.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given an authorized Moderator and case within Community scope, when allowed actions are requested, then the server returns only actions permitted by current role, policy, target, and state.
2. Given the actor is the reporter, target, subject, close participant, prior decision maker for an independent Appeal, or otherwise conflicted under policy, when decision is attempted, then reassignment or higher approval is required and state remains unchanged.
3. Given a Community Moderator attempts a platform-wide action or action against a protected higher authority, when requested, then it is denied and cannot be achieved through bulk, API, or support paths.
4. Given a high-impact action requires additional approval or stronger verification, when missing, then the server returns remediation and commits no Moderation Action.

### States and accessibility

Explain allowed action scope, conflict, reassignment, approval, and stronger-verification states;
disabled affordances are informative but server enforcement remains authoritative.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: authority matrix, target hierarchy, conflict, approval, and verification rules.
- Integration/contract: role/target combinations, API/bulk bypass attempts, and reassignment.
- Component: allowed actions, conflict, approval, and remediation states.
- Journey/live: exercise Community, platform, conflicted, and high-impact decision paths.
- Manual/rendered: keyboard, screen-reader explanation, destructive distinction, and copy review.

## L2-SAFE-008: Apply one auditable Moderation Action

**Traces to:** L1-SAFE-003

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Warnings, labels, limits, locks, hides/removals, suspensions, Community bans, and platform bans are
typed, reasoned, scoped, time-bounded where applicable, and committed atomically with an Audit Event.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given authorized decision, current target version, policy basis, reason, scope, duration, and accepted effect-matrix version, when applied, then one Moderation Action and Audit Event commit with actor, target, bound matrix version, resolved effect summary, before/after state, and correlation.
2. Given stale state, illegal action, insufficient scope, invalid duration, or missing required reason, when submitted, then a typed failure is returned and target, case, and projections remain unchanged.
3. Given the action changes access or visibility, when post-commit processing runs, then direct URL, Feed, Search, Message, Event, Notification, realtime, cache, export, and integration paths reconcile without exposing retained evidence.
4. Given processing is retried, when the same decision identity is seen, then no duplicate Action, duration, notice, or projection effect is created.
5. Given a later effect-matrix version changes an existing Action type, when migration is evaluated, then the Action remains on its bound version by default; a restriction-reducing repair is audited and communicated, while any material expansion creates a new linked Action, notice, and fresh Appeal eligibility rather than silently strengthening the old decision.

### States and accessibility

Show action impact preview, reason/policy, duration, stronger approval, submitting, conflict, applied,
and partial-projection warning; destructive severity is explicit and not color-only.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: action matrix, duration, target version, idempotency, and audit requirements.
- Integration/contract: apply/reject, atomicity, outbox, and every affected projection boundary.
- Component: impact preview, confirmation, conflict, and applied state.
- Journey/live: apply each action class and prove authoritative behavior across affected surfaces.
- Manual/rendered: keyboard, screen reader, high contrast, reason quality, and destructive review.

## L2-SAFE-009: Notify, expire, and reverse Actions safely

**Traces to:** L1-SAFE-003

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Affected people receive the permitted policy basis, impact, duration, and review path; expiry or
reversal restores only state justified by the Action and current independent restrictions.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given a subject-facing Action commits, when notice is due, then the eligible Account receives a privacy-safe explanation, effective scope, duration, applicable policy, and Appeal or support path.
2. Given notice could create safety risk, reveal a reporter, or disclose restricted evidence, when rendered, then those details are redacted while the decision remains understandable.
3. Given a temporary Action expires or an authorized reversal commits, when reconciliation runs, then only that Action's restrictions are removed; other Blocks, Actions, Membership, or deletion state remain effective.
4. Given expiry or reversal processing fails or repeats, when recovered, then state converges idempotently and operators are alerted to overdue restrictions or projections.

### States and accessibility

Subject views show active, expiring, expired, reversed, appeal-eligible, and unavailable states using
plain language, exact localized time, semantic structure, and safe support links.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: notice disclosure, expiry, reversal, and independent-restriction rules.
- Integration/contract: notice Delivery, scheduler, replay, projection repair, and overdue alert.
- Component: Action explanation, countdown/date, Appeal link, and restored-state presentation.
- Journey/live: apply a temporary Action, add another restriction, expire it, and verify only its effect lifts.
- Manual/rendered: subject safety, locale/time zone, keyboard, screen reader, and plain-language review.

## L2-SAFE-010: Adjudicate eligible Appeals and repair state

**Traces to:** L1-SAFE-004

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

An eligible subject can submit one bounded Appeal for each materially distinct Action through a
strongly verified ordinary or restricted-remediation Session. The deadline begins only when the
notice is durably available through an allowed channel, and an appropriately independent reviewer can
uphold, reduce, replace, or overturn the Action with complete history and repair work.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given an appealable Action whose notice is durably available, when the subject proves Account control through an ordinary or restricted-remediation Session and submits bounded grounds before the measured deadline, then one Appeal is committed and safely acknowledged without exposing restricted case details.
2. Given notice Delivery fails or the subject cannot access every allowed notice channel, when the deadline is calculated, then it does not expire before the configured minimum window after durable notice availability or a documented safety-operator remedy.
3. Given an eligible independent reviewer adjudicates, when the outcome commits, then rationale, reviewed evidence, policy, actor, timestamps, and uphold/modify/overturn result are audited.
4. Given an Action is modified or overturned, when repair runs, then request and queued-work authorization changes before the next effect, derived projections reconcile within the accepted Action-repair objective, default five minutes, each prior notice follows the versioned correction policy with a recorded outcome, and unrelated restrictions remain.
5. Given adjudication increases duration, scope, severity, or restrictions materially, when it commits, then a new linked Moderation Action and notice are created with a fresh Appeal eligibility decision and deadline rather than making the harsher outcome unappealable.

### States and accessibility

Show eligibility, deadline, submitting, acknowledged, under review, upheld, modified, overturned,
repair-pending, and closed states without revealing reviewer or reporter private information.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: eligibility, notice-anchored deadline, restricted Session, independence, outcome, and repair rules.
- Integration/contract: restricted sign-in, Delivery failure, submit, duplicate/expired, adjudicate, new Action, Audit Event, and projection repair.
- Component: Appeal form, status timeline, outcome, and repair-pending states.
- Journey/live: appeal and overturn an Action, proving restoration and unrelated restriction preservation.
- Manual/rendered: keyboard, screen reader, localized deadline, cognitive load, and copy review.

## L2-SAFE-011: Govern automation and emergency escalation

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Automated signals may prioritize or recommend but cannot silently impose high-impact final Actions;

2. Given a proposed high-impact Action depends on automation, when approval is requested, then an authorized human reviews relevant evidence and remains accountable for the final decision.
3. Given automation is unavailable, degraded, biased beyond an accepted threshold, or produces excessive errors, when a kill switch is applied, then manual reporting and case processing continue without losing cases.
4. Given an urgent safety or potentially unlawful-content category is identified, when escalated, then only trained authorized roles follow the current runbook, preserve required evidence, record actions, and avoid unsupported compliance claims.

### States and accessibility

Reviewer views label automated signals, uncertainty, degraded status, human decision, and emergency
path in text; urgent styling does not flash, overwhelm, or rely on color alone.

### Verification

- Component: signal explanation, uncertainty, kill switch, and escalation states.

## L2-SAFE-013: Enforce the Moderation Action effect matrix

**Traces to:** L1-SAFE-003

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

A versioned server-owned matrix defines the exact effect of every warning, label, limit, lock,
hide/removal, suspension, Community ban, and platform ban by target, scope, lifecycle, and action.
Every Action binds a matrix version and every request/background effect evaluates that binding plus
current independent state; no feature invents independent ban semantics. A platform-banned Account
may receive only a strongly verified restricted-remediation Session for listed non-participatory actions.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given a current Moderation Action, when sign-in, Session renewal, join/request/invitation, protected read/write, Post, Comment, Reaction, Message, Event/RSVP, billing, privacy, Feed, Search, cache, export, realtime, Job, Notification, Webhook, or integration processing occurs, then the Action's bound matrix version returns and enforces one typed allowed, denied, filtered, or remediation-only outcome together with current independent restrictions.
2. Given a platform ban becomes active, when existing Sessions and later sign-in are evaluated, then ordinary Sessions are revoked or denied and stronger Account proof can issue only a bounded restricted-remediation Session for Appeal, security recovery, billing cancellation/history, privacy export/deletion, and approved support actions.
3. Given a Community ban becomes active, when the Account uses any identifier, invitation, request, existing Membership, alternate Space, direct route, bulk API, realtime subscription, or queued work for that Community, then participation and prohibited disclosure stop while unrelated Community access follows its own state.
4. Given a worker or Delivery was queued before the Action, when it executes, then current Action, Membership, visibility, Block, and deletion state are re-evaluated before any private or external effect.
5. Given an Action expires, is reversed, or is replaced, when reconciliation runs, then only effects owned by that Action change and independent Actions, Blocks, Membership state, privacy choices, and evidence access remain intact.

### States and accessibility

Ordinary, restricted-remediation, action-limited, banned, expired, and restored states name the allowed
next action without exposing reporters or evidence; sign-in and remediation remain keyboard and
screen-reader operable.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Test every Action type against every matrix surface and independent-restriction precedence.
- Integration/contract: Run the full role/resource/action matrix across HTTP, hubs, Jobs, Deliveries, billing, privacy, and integrations.
- Component: Test denied, filtered, restricted-remediation, Appeal, billing, privacy, and restored states.
- Journey/live: Apply Community and platform bans during active Sessions and queued work, then Appeal and reverse.
- Manual/rendered: Safety, security, privacy, billing, and accessibility review of the published matrix.

## L2-SAFE-014: Route punitive changes through Moderation Actions

**Traces to:** L1-SAFE-003

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Voluntary departure, ordinary ownership cleanup, author deletion, archival, and neutral
administrative correction remain distinct from punitive enforcement. Any Membership suspension or
removal, content removal, restriction, or bulk change whose purpose is policy enforcement must invoke
the Moderation Case decision path and create one typed Moderation Action with notice and Appeal rules.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given an Account voluntarily leaves, an author performs an allowed deletion, or an administrator fixes non-punitive configuration, when the ordinary lifecycle use case commits, then it records that reason class and creates no implied warning, ban, suspension, or Moderation Action.
2. Given an actor selects a policy-violation, safety, abuse, or punitive reason, when a Membership, content, Event, Message, Profile, or bulk operation is submitted outside the moderation path, then the server rejects it and preserves state with a link to the authorized Case workflow.
3. Given an authorized punitive decision, when it commits, then Case, policy version, evidence, conflict/recusal result, approvals, typed Action, before/after state, notice rule, Appeal eligibility, actor, and Audit Event are joined by stable identities.
4. Given a bulk operation contains punitive targets, when preview and execution run, then each target receives its own eligible Case/Action result and one failure cannot be hidden behind an administrative aggregate.

### States and accessibility

Administrative interfaces distinguish voluntary, neutral, and enforcement purposes before
confirmation; punitive actions expose Case, policy, notice, Appeal, scope, and per-target outcomes in
accessible text.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Test reason classes, enforcement routing, Case/Action requirements, and bulk target policy.
- Integration/contract: Probe Membership/content/admin/API/bulk paths for punitive bypass and atomic linkage.
- Component: Test purpose selection, rejected bypass, Case handoff, and per-target outcomes.
- Journey/live: Attempt enforcement from ordinary lifecycle and bulk tools, then complete it through a Case.
- Manual/rendered: Moderator, administrator, legal-policy, and accessibility review of distinctions.

## L2-SAFE-015: Resolve enforcement and ownership continuity atomically

**Traces to:** L1-SAFE-003

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Safety authority cannot be blocked by last-owner or last-manager invariants, and enforcement cannot
leave a Community ownerless. Before an Action disables the last eligible owner, organizer, manager,
billing contact, or other required controller, one reviewed transaction transfers control to an
eligible accepted successor, establishes time-bounded platform stewardship, or enters the exact
Community/dependent-resource retirement or terminal lifecycle defined by its owning capability.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given an Action targets a controller and an eligible accepted successor exists, when enforcement commits, then required authority transfers before the target loses access and both transitions share the Case, Action, and Audit correlation.
2. Given no eligible successor exists and immediate enforcement is required, when an authorized independent platform safety approval commits, then tightly scoped stewardship receives only the controls needed to secure, transfer, or close the resource, with purpose, expiry, reviewer, and complete Audit Events.
3. Given neither transfer nor stewardship is allowed for a Community, when enforcement commits, then it enters `retirement-pending` with the dependency manifest, mutation freeze, permitted remediation, cancellation boundary, and final retention behavior of the Community lifecycle rather than inventing a separate closed state or retaining an active banned controller.
4. Given the affected controller owns a Space, Event, Conversation, billing subject, invitation, Webhook Endpoint, or other dependent resource, when its terminal resolution commits, then the registered type-specific archive, cancel, close, transfer, disable, settlement, retention, and queued-work rules complete atomically or the Action remains continuity-pending without falsely claiming enforcement completion.
5. Given stewardship expires, the Action reverses, or a successor accepts, when continuity is reevaluated, then temporary authority is removed and cannot become an ordinary self-granted operator or Membership Role.

### States and accessibility

Safety tools show continuity blocker, successor acceptance, stewardship scope/expiry, closure impact,
approval, conflict, and completed states with explicit focus-managed confirmation.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Test last-controller precedence, successor, stewardship, closure, expiry, and reversal rules.
- Integration/contract: Race enforcement with transfer/demotion and verify one atomic safe outcome.
- Component: Test continuity preview, approval, stewardship, closure, and remediation states.
- Journey/live: Ban a sole owner and sole Event organizer through each configured resolution path.
- Manual/rendered: Safety, security, billing, privacy, governance, and accessibility review.

## L2-SAFE-016: Preserve a bounded prior-view reporting path

**Traces to:** L1-SAFE-001

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Supported authenticated views may issue an opaque Report Receipt that proves the viewer, target,
exact version, and authorized-view time while retaining only the minimum protected evidence needed
to keep reporting possible through the disclosed receipt window.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given an authenticated Account is authorized to view a supported reportable target, when that exact version is disclosed, then a Report Receipt binds Account, target type/identity/version, policy context, view time, expiry, and a protected evidence reference; any client bearer value is random, purpose-bound, and stored only through a non-recoverable verifier.
2. Given the target uses persisted version history, when a receipt is issued, then that exact protected version is pinned only until receipt expiry or accepted Report evidence retention takes over; if no durable version exists, a minimized encrypted snapshot is created under the same access, expiry, and purpose policy before the view is represented as receipt-backed.
3. Given a target is later edited, hidden, deleted, Blocked, or made inaccessible, when the bound Account submits the unexpired receipt, then the server verifies it without reconstructing from current state and can atomically commit the Report plus exact evidence; another Account, target, version, purpose, or altered token cannot use it.
4. Given a receipt expires, is consumed under the single-Report policy, or its retained version is no longer permitted by a higher-priority legal/safety rule, when cleanup runs, then the verifier and unneeded pinned snapshot/reference are deleted idempotently and no target identity remains discoverable through receipt probes.
5. Given receipt issuance or evidence pinning would violate an accepted data-minimization, safety, capacity, or retention rule, when the target is viewed, then ordinary authorized viewing may continue but no prior-view reporting promise is shown; current-view reporting and urgent support paths remain explicit.

### States and accessibility

Reporting controls distinguish current-view, recent-view available, expired/unavailable, submitting,
and acknowledged states without exposing receipt tokens, target retention, or Block ownership.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Test binding, expiry, verifier, version pin, consumption, precedence, and cleanup rules.
- Integration/contract: Issue on each target type, edit/delete/Block, cross-Account replay, report, and expire.
- Component: Current/recent reporting availability, expired-safe result, submit, and acknowledgement.
- Journey/live: View a Message/Event/Post, remove access, report exact prior versions, then expire receipts.
- Manual/rendered: Privacy disclosure, distress-aware copy, keyboard, screen reader, and safe failure review.
