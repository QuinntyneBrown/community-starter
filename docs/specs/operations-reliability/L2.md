# OPER — Operations and reliability

## L2-OPER-001: Correlate structured operational telemetry

**Traces to:** L1-OPER-001

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

The API, Application handlers, persistence, external clients, Jobs, and realtime adapters emit
structured events with stable names, severity, UTC time, environment, service version, and propagated
correlation and trace context. Telemetry follows the privacy allow-list and never uses content bodies
as diagnostic context.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given a request causes database work, a Job, and an external call, when an operator follows its correlation or trace, then each boundary and final outcome can be reconstructed without reading private payloads.
2. Given work resumes asynchronously or on another instance, when it executes, then originating correlation and a new execution identity are both available.
3. Given unbounded or sensitive input reaches a failure path, when telemetry emits, then approved classifications appear but secrets, tokens, content, Messages, and raw personal fields do not.
4. Given telemetry export is unavailable, when application work continues, then bounded buffering or dropping cannot exhaust the service and the loss is measurable.

### States and accessibility

Operator views expose missing spans, sampling, delayed ingestion, and exporter failure in text; trace
navigation is keyboard accessible and does not require color discrimination.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Test event schema, redaction, correlation propagation, and cardinality guards.
- Integration/contract: Trace request, database, Job, realtime, and external boundaries through configured sinks.
- Component: Test operator trace and diagnostic states where supplied.
- Journey/live: Follow a successful and failed critical journey across all processes.
- Manual/rendered: Audit sample telemetry for privacy, readability, and stable naming.

## L2-OPER-002: Measure technical and product health

**Traces to:** L1-OPER-001

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Metrics cover latency, throughput, failure, and saturation plus critical product transitions and
queues such as Membership activation, content publication, Search freshness, Feed freshness, media
processing, Notification Delivery, provider feedback, and Moderation Case age. Dimensions are bounded
and privacy safe.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given representative successful and failed operations, when metrics are queried, then totals and outcome counts equal committed results and latency or saturation aggregates remain within the numeric accuracy tolerance recorded in the versioned metric definition.
2. Given a product transition rolls back, when metrics emit, then it is not counted as a committed success.
3. Given a dimension contains an Account, resource, free-form, URL, or other high-cardinality value, when metric validation runs, then that dimension is rejected or transformed to an approved bounded class.
4. Given a queue or index crosses its freshness objective, when metrics evaluate it, then age and backlog are visible without exposing queued content.

### States and accessibility

Metric summaries pair charts with text/table alternatives, define units and time zones, and distinguish
no data, delayed data, healthy, warning, and breached states.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Test outcome classification, histogram boundaries, and dimension allow-lists.
- Integration/contract: Compare controlled workload outcomes with exported metrics.
- Component: Verify chart alternatives and no-data/breach states.
- Journey/live: Generate a critical transition and backlog and observe the declared signals.
- Manual/rendered: Review metric semantics, privacy, units, and accessibility.

## L2-OPER-003: Distinguish liveness, readiness, and dependency health

**Traces to:** L1-OPER-001

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Health endpoints distinguish a running process from readiness to receive traffic. Readiness checks
only dependencies required for safe request handling, use strict timeouts, return machine-readable
status without secrets, and do not amplify a dependency outage.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given the process event loop is responsive, when liveness is requested, then it succeeds without depending on the database or external providers.
2. Given an essential dependency cannot serve required work, when readiness is requested, then the instance is not ready and the response identifies only a safe dependency class.
3. Given an optional provider is unavailable, when readiness is evaluated, then core traffic remains ready if the declared degradation policy is safe and provider health is exposed separately.
4. Given probes run at configured frequency during an outage, when dependencies remain slow, then probe timeouts and concurrency do not materially worsen the outage.

### States and accessibility

Human health views label live, ready, degraded, not ready, unknown, and stale data, with semantic tables
and non-color status.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Test health aggregation and required/optional classifications.
- Integration/contract: Fail each dependency and verify probe status, bounds, and safe output.
- Component: Test health summary rendering where supplied.
- Journey/live: Remove and restore an essential and optional dependency under traffic.
- Manual/rendered: Review orchestration settings and operator interpretation.

## L2-OPER-004: Provide owned operational dashboards

**Traces to:** L1-OPER-001

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Each service objective and critical subsystem has a version-controlled or reproducibly configured
dashboard that identifies owner, source, units, expected range, deploy markers, and links to alerts
and runbooks. Dashboard access is least privilege and does not expose Community content.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given an operator opens a critical dashboard, when data is current, then service-level, dependency, resource, queue, and recent-release context required for triage is available.
2. Given a query or data source changes incompatibly, when validation runs, then dashboard drift is detected before or during release rather than silently showing no data.
3. Given an unauthorized person requests a dashboard, when access is evaluated, then operational and Community dimensions are not disclosed.
4. Given data is delayed or absent, when the dashboard renders, then it identifies staleness instead of implying zero activity.

### States and accessibility

Dashboards provide text/table equivalents for material charts, labeled axes and units, keyboard
navigation, zoom support, and explicit loading, stale, error, and no-data states.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Validate dashboard metadata and required panels as configuration.
- Integration/contract: Verify data-source queries and access controls.
- Component: Test stale/error/no-data and accessible alternatives.
- Journey/live: Use a dashboard to diagnose a controlled failure and release marker.
- Manual/rendered: Operator and accessibility review of each launch-critical dashboard.

## L2-OPER-005: Define and report service objectives

**Traces to:** L1-OPER-002

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Before launch, owners define service-level indicators and objectives for availability, request and
Job latency, critical journey success, Feed and Search freshness, and Notification timeliness. Each
objective states population, window, exclusions, data source, error budget, and response policy.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given a proposed objective lacks population, calculation, window, exclusions, owner, or evidence source, when readiness is reviewed, then it cannot support a production-readiness claim.
2. Given qualifying events occur, when the objective is calculated, then independent recomputation from source data reaches the same categorical status and a numeric value within the calculation tolerance recorded in the accepted objective definition.
3. Given an error budget crosses its policy threshold, when release decisions are evaluated, then the documented reliability response is triggered and recorded.
4. Given planned maintenance is excluded, when reporting occurs, then the exclusion is narrow, declared in advance where possible, and visible rather than deleting raw failures.

### States and accessibility

Objective reports define units and status in text, include no/insufficient-data states, and provide
accessible alternatives to charts.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Test SLI calculation, windows, exclusions, and budget rules.
- Integration/contract: Recompute objectives from controlled telemetry.
- Component: Test objective status and insufficient-data presentation.
- Journey/live: Deliberately breach a non-production objective and observe policy activation.
- Manual/rendered: Stakeholder review targets, exclusions, and ownership.

## L2-OPER-006: Enforce performance and resource budgets

**Traces to:** L1-OPER-002

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

The product defines representative budgets for marketing first render, Angular route loading,
critical API latency, query count and size, media handling, memory, CPU, outbound calls, and Job
duration. Inputs, pages, concurrency, timeouts, and payloads are bounded at every untrusted boundary.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given a representative production build and data set, when budget tests run, then each measured path reports its value and fails the gate when an accepted threshold is exceeded.
2. Given an input, page, upload, response, external call, or concurrent operation exceeds its bound, when handled, then the server rejects or truncates it according to contract without unbounded allocation or partial mutation.
3. Given a query path grows with page size or relationship depth unexpectedly, when performance checks run, then query count or execution evidence detects the regression.
4. Given a budget changes, when accepted, then evidence, user effect, operational cost, and new threshold are recorded rather than silently updating the gate.

### States and accessibility

User-facing timeout and bounded-result states preserve input where safe, explain next action, support
keyboard retry, and avoid repeated live-region noise.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Test all validation, size, concurrency, and timeout bounds.
- Integration/contract: Profile representative endpoints, queries, uploads, and external calls.
- Component: Test timeout, partial, limited, and retry states.
- Journey/live: Measure core journey and marketing budgets from a published artifact.
- Manual/rendered: Review performance report and perceived behavior on constrained devices/networks.

## L2-OPER-008: Degrade safely during dependency failure

**Traces to:** L1-OPER-002

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Every required and optional dependency has a timeout, failure classification, user effect, retry or
queue behavior, recovery/reconciliation rule, and owner. Core authorization and privacy never fail
open; REST and persisted state remain authoritative when realtime, Search, analytics, or delivery is
degraded.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given authorization, identity, or authoritative persistence cannot establish a safe answer, when a protected action is requested, then it fails closed without mutation.
2. Given realtime or Search is unavailable, when permitted content is used, then supported REST or direct navigation continues where safe and the client identifies stale/degraded results.
3. Given an external Delivery provider fails, when product state commits, then the state remains committed and retryable Delivery status is visible without blocking the originating request.
4. Given a dependency recovers, when reconciliation runs, then missed projections or work converge without duplicate effects or unauthorized disclosure.

### States and accessibility

Degraded banners and inline states explain affected capability, freshness, retry, and alternative path;
they preserve focus, use restrained announcements, and do not rely on color.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Test required/optional classification and fail-closed decisions.
- Integration/contract: Inject each dependency failure and recovery across request and Job paths.
- Component: Test offline, stale, delayed, retry, and recovered states.
- Journey/live: Complete a supported journey with realtime, Search, or delivery unavailable.
- Manual/rendered: Review runbook, user copy, and safety of each degradation mode.

## L2-OPER-009: Route actionable alerts to an owner

**Traces to:** L1-OPER-003

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Alerts correspond to an observable user or operational risk, declare severity, threshold, evaluation
window, owner, notification path, runbook, deduplication, silence limits, and resolution signal. Alerts
contain safe context and are tested without paging from untrusted environments.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given a monitored condition crosses its sustained threshold, when evaluation runs, then one deduplicated alert reaches the current owner with severity, environment, dashboard, and runbook.
2. Given the condition recovers, when the resolution rule is met, then the alert resolves and records duration without requiring manual metric deletion.
3. Given an alert is silenced, when its maximum window or ownership expires, then it becomes active or requires reviewed renewal.
4. Given a pull request or development environment produces a signal, when routing evaluates it, then production on-call is not paged unless an explicit test path is active.

### States and accessibility

Alert consoles expose firing, acknowledged, silenced, resolved, stale, and unowned states with
keyboard-accessible actions and redundant status cues.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Validate alert metadata, routing, silence, and resolution rules.
- Integration/contract: Trigger each launch-critical alert through its real evaluation path.
- Component: Test alert state and acknowledgement UI where supplied.
- Journey/live: Conduct an alert-to-owner drill in staging.
- Manual/rendered: On-call review actionability, noise risk, and safe context.

## L2-OPER-010: Run and review incidents consistently

**Traces to:** L1-OPER-003

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

An incident process defines severity, command and technical roles, secure coordination, customer and
status communication, evidence preservation, escalation for security/privacy/safety events,
resolution criteria, and blameless follow-up with owned actions.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given an event meets an incident threshold, when declared, then severity, commander, timeline, affected capabilities, communication owner, and secure workspace are established.
2. Given the incident affects data confidentiality, integrity, safety, or availability, when triaged, then the corresponding specialist path and evidence controls are activated.
3. Given service is restored, when the incident closes, then impact, detection, response, contributing conditions, evidence, and owned time-bounded follow-ups are recorded.
4. Given a follow-up expires incomplete, when review runs, then its risk is re-owned or explicitly accepted rather than silently disappearing.

### States and accessibility

Incident tools and templates support high-contrast, keyboard use, clear UTC/local timestamps, and
concise text alternatives for visual timelines.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Validate required incident and follow-up metadata.
- Integration/contract: Verify paging, status, audit, and secure-evidence integrations.
- Component: Test incident status, timelines, and error states where productized.
- Journey/live: Run tabletop exercises for outage, abuse, and data incidents.
- Manual/rendered: Review exercise findings, accessibility, and action closure.

## L2-OPER-011: Maintain executable operational runbooks

**Traces to:** L1-OPER-003

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Versioned runbooks cover deployment and rollback, migration failure, database outage, queue backlog,
Search rebuild, media or delivery provider outage, secret rotation, certificate expiry, abuse event,
data incident, and restore. Each states trigger, access, safe diagnostics, decision points,
verification, rollback or forward-fix, escalation, and owner.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given a named operational scenario occurs, when an on-call operator opens its runbook, then prerequisites, bounded commands or controls, expected evidence, stop conditions, and escalation are current.
2. Given a runbook command or link changes, when documentation validation runs, then broken paths or unsafe placeholders fail the gate.
3. Given a runbook is exercised, when the drill ends, then result, duration, divergence, and required updates are recorded.
4. Given an operation could expose or destroy data, when the runbook reaches it, then scope verification, approval, recovery, and post-action evidence are explicit.

### States and accessibility

Runbooks use semantic headings, copy-safe bounded commands, warnings adjacent to actions, text-based
decision paths, and formats usable during narrow-screen or low-connectivity response.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Validate required runbook metadata and review dates.
- Integration/contract: Check links, commands, permissions, and referenced alert/dashboard identifiers.
- Component: Not applicable unless runbooks have a UI; validate rendered documentation instead.
- Journey/live: Execute each launch-critical runbook in a disposable or staging environment.
- Manual/rendered: On-call review for correctness, safety, and scanability.

## L2-OPER-012: Create and verify protected backups

**Traces to:** L1-OPER-004

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Authoritative relational data, required object metadata and content, and essential configuration have
encrypted, access-controlled backups with declared frequency, retention, immutability where needed,

3. Given an unauthorized workload or operator requests backup bytes or keys, when access is evaluated, then it is denied and the attempt is audited.
4. Given backup retention expires without a valid hold, when lifecycle processing runs, then the artifact and obsolete keys follow the declared destruction policy.

accessible tables and exact timestamps.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Test schedule, age, retention, and verification-state policy.
- Integration/contract: Create, inspect, deny unauthorized access to, and expire backup artifacts.
- Component: Test safe aggregate backup status.
- Journey/live: Run scheduled and failed backups in a production-like environment.
- Manual/rendered: Security and operations review encryption, key ownership, and alerts.

## L2-OPER-013: Prove recovery objectives through restore drills

**Related:** —

Accepted recovery point and recovery time objectives identify covered failure scenarios, dependencies,

3. Given measured recovery point or time misses its objective, when the drill closes, then readiness remains qualified and a time-bounded owner/action is recorded.
4. Given the drill environment is retired, when cleanup runs, then restored data, credentials, network access, and temporary artifacts are removed under audit.

### States and accessibility

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Validate objective calculations and drill completion rules.

- Component: Test drill status and accessible reports where supplied.

## L2-OPER-014: Reconcile derived state after recovery

**Traces to:** L1-OPER-004

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Recovery has a deterministic procedure for pending Jobs, outbox events, Search, caches, Feeds,
realtime connections, Attachments, Notifications, email-provider feedback, and deletions that
occurred after the recovery point. Derived systems rebuild from authorized source state and do not
become independent truth.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given authoritative data is restored, when derived rebuild runs, then Search, Feed, cache, and media projections contain only currently authorized source records.
2. Given committed outbox or Job records are present at the recovery point, when workers resume, then leases and idempotency prevent duplicate user-visible or external-Delivery effects.
3. Given a deletion or security action occurred after the recovery point, when recovery reconciles external records, then the documented incident procedure prevents the restored copy from silently returning to ordinary service.
4. Given reconciliation is incomplete, when traffic enablement is evaluated, then affected capabilities remain unavailable or visibly degraded rather than serving unsafe stale state.

### States and accessibility

Maintenance and recovery UI names unavailable, read-only, rebuilding, stale, and restored states,
offers accessible status updates, and avoids indefinite animated indicators.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Test replay, idempotency, and readiness gates.
- Integration/contract: Restore with pending Jobs/events and rebuild all configured projections.
- Component: Test maintenance and reconciliation states.
- Journey/live: Exercise critical reads and mutations before, during, and after a recovery drill.
- Manual/rendered: Review recovery sequencing and user communication.

## L2-OPER-015: Operate migrations and backfills safely

**Traces to:** L1-OPER-005

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Database changes use checked-in forward migrations, compatibility-aware expand/backfill/contract
steps when needed, reviewed query and lock impact, backup/recovery prerequisites, observable progress,
and an explicit rollback or forward-fix path. Applied shared-environment migrations are immutable.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given a schema change, when gates run, then a clean database and representative prior version both migrate to the expected schema and application behavior.
2. Given old and new artifacts overlap during rollout, when each accesses the expanding schema, then the documented compatibility window preserves correct behavior.
3. Given a backfill processes production-scale data, when it runs, then batches, cancellation, checkpoints, throttling, failure, and progress are bounded and observable.
4. Given migration verification fails, when deployment evaluates health, then traffic promotion stops and the recorded recovery path is used without editing applied migration history.

### States and accessibility

Operator output names pending, applying, backfilling, validating, completed, failed, and intervention
states with exact versions and accessible logs/status.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Test backfill transformation and checkpoint rules.
- Integration/contract: Apply clean, upgrade, concurrent-version, interruption, and resume scenarios on the real provider.
- Component: Test operator migration status if provided.
- Journey/live: Rehearse the production migration and recovery path on representative data.
- Manual/rendered: Review SQL/plan impact, backup evidence, ownership, and runbook.

## L2-OPER-016: Operate durable Job queues

**Traces to:** L1-OPER-005

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Jobs persist intent and lifecycle, use multi-instance-safe leases, bounded attempts and exponential
backoff with jitter, idempotent handlers, cancellation and shutdown, per-kind concurrency, terminal
failure storage, operator visibility, and safe replay. Workers reauthorize time-sensitive user effects.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given two workers claim the same Job, when lease coordination runs, then at most one active execution owns it and lease recovery does not duplicate committed effects.
2. Given a transient failure occurs, when attempts remain, then the Job retries after bounded backoff; a permanent or exhausted failure enters a visible terminal state.
3. Given a recipient, resource, Permission, Block, or preference changes after enqueue, when the Job would cause an external or private effect, then current authorization and policy are re-evaluated.
4. Given a worker receives shutdown, when grace time permits, then it completes or releases work safely without accepting unbounded new Jobs.

### States and accessibility

Operator views expose scheduled, ready, leased, running, retrying, cancelled, succeeded, and terminal
states with accessible filters, safe errors, and confirmed replay.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Test leases, retries, classification, authorization refresh, and state transitions.
- Integration/contract: Race workers, kill executions, advance clocks, and replay on real persistence.
- Component: Test queue views and destructive replay/cancel controls.
- Journey/live: Interrupt and recover a critical Delivery and deletion Job.
- Manual/rendered: Review backlog runbook, privacy, and operator safeguards.
