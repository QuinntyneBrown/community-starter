# PRIV — Privacy and data lifecycle

## L2-PRIV-001: Maintain a personal-data inventory

**Traces to:** L1-PRIV-001

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Every personal-data category has an owner, subjects, source, purpose, sensitivity classification,
authoritative store, derived destinations, recipients, retention rule, deletion behavior, and
production access policy. CI or release review detects an undeclared category or destination.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given a feature proposes a new personal-data field or destination, when it is reviewed, then its inventory entry and lifecycle rule are required before release.
2. Given an operator inspects a data category, when the inventory is opened, then its authoritative and derived locations, processors, purpose, owner, and retention rule are identifiable.
3. Given a data flow is no longer used, when its purpose expires, then collection stops and existing copies enter the declared deletion or anonymization flow.

### States and accessibility

The internal inventory supports empty, overdue-review, invalid, and unavailable states; sensitive
values are never used as examples, and review controls are keyboard operable with named status.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Validate inventory schema and review-expiry policy.
- Integration/contract: Verify configured stores and processors map to declared entries.
- Component: Verify review and validation states for authorized operators.
- Journey/live: Add a representative data field and prove the release gate rejects it until declared.
- Manual/rendered: Review inventory readability, access restrictions, and ownership contacts.

## L2-PRIV-002: Obtain and honor optional-processing choices

**Traces to:** L1-PRIV-001

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Optional analytics, personalization, marketing, and embeds remain off until the applicable Account or
Visitor makes a granular, recorded choice. Withdrawal is as direct as opt-in and stops future
processing without disabling necessary service behavior.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given no optional-processing choice exists, when a Visitor or Account uses the platform, then optional scripts, cookies, events, and provider calls do not run.
2. Given a person accepts selected purposes, when the choice is committed, then only those purposes run and the policy version, time, locale, and choice are recorded.
3. Given a person withdraws a purpose, when the change is committed, then future collection and delivery for that purpose stop and necessary processing remains available.
4. Given the preference service is unavailable, when consent cannot be established, then the platform uses the restrictive choice and still exposes necessary privacy controls.

### States and accessibility

Choices use equally prominent accept, reject, and customize actions; focus, labels, descriptions, and
status announcements work at narrow widths and without motion or color alone.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Prove purpose-choice evaluation and restrictive fallback.
- Integration/contract: Verify cookie, analytics, embed, and provider gates consume current choices.
- Component: Test initial, customized, saved, withdrawn, and failed-save states.
- Journey/live: Change choices and inspect subsequent browser storage and network activity.
- Manual/rendered: Review plain-language copy and keyboard/screen-reader flow.

## L2-PRIV-003: Apply privacy-preserving defaults

**Traces to:** L1-PRIV-001

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

New Accounts, Profiles, Communities, and optional capabilities begin with the least exposure that
still supports the selected primary journey. A person must deliberately broaden discoverability,
messaging, presence, or analytics visibility.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given a new Account has not changed privacy settings, when another person searches, messages, or inspects presence, then only the documented minimum is disclosed.
2. Given a setting would broaden an audience, when it is changed, then the UI names the resulting audience and the server validates that the Account may make the change.
3. Given a Community policy requires a stricter setting, when a Member attempts to weaken it, then the server rejects the request and preserves the stricter state.

### States and accessibility

Privacy controls name audiences in text, expose inherited and locked states, confirm material
expansion, and remain operable with keyboard, zoom, and screen readers.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Test default, inherited, and policy-bounded visibility calculations.
- Integration/contract: Verify new-record defaults and forbidden broadening responses.
- Component: Test audience explanations, inherited state, and conflict reconciliation.
- Journey/live: Create an Account and prove discoverability and contact defaults from a second Account.
- Manual/rendered: Inspect comprehension at representative widths and contrast modes.

## L2-PRIV-004: Gate participation where age eligibility is unresolved

**Traces to:** L1-PRIV-001

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Each product specialization records its intended audience, markets, minimum-age policy, and any
required guardian process. Where eligibility must be known, the server prevents self-service
participation until it is established without exposing age data unnecessarily.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given the configured market requires an eligibility decision, when eligibility is unknown or not met, then Account participation is blocked and no Community Membership is activated.
2. Given an eligible person completes the approved flow, when the result is committed, then the Account-bound eligibility result, policy version, decision time, expiry/review time, and minimum evidence class or protected provider reference required to prove the decision are retained while raw age or guardian documents are discarded unless the reviewed policy requires them.
3. Given no reviewed age policy exists for a target market, when production enablement is attempted, then startup or release validation fails with a non-sensitive diagnostic.

### States and accessibility

Eligibility screens avoid coercive patterns, explain unavailable outcomes without revealing sensitive
facts to other users, and support keyboard, screen readers, zoom, and localized content.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Test eligibility policy, transitions, and minimized retained result.
- Integration/contract: Verify membership and production-enable gates.
- Component: Test unknown, ineligible, pending, eligible, and service-error states.
- Journey/live: Exercise the configured eligibility journey and a bypass attempt.
- Manual/rendered: Obtain privacy and accessibility review of copy and data minimization.

## L2-PRIV-005: Correct controllable personal data

**Traces to:** L1-PRIV-002

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

An authenticated Account holder can inspect and correct user-managed personal data. Security
attributes, verified identifiers, audit history, and disputed records use their governed workflows
rather than arbitrary inline editing.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given recent authentication and valid values, when an Account holder changes controllable data, then the authoritative record and affected projections use the new value.
2. Given a verified or security-sensitive value changes, when the request is made, then its verification and notification workflow runs before authority moves to the new value.
3. Given another Account attempts the correction, when the server authorizes the request, then it returns a scoped forbidden or unavailable response and state is unchanged.

### States and accessibility

Forms provide current, validation, verifying, saved, conflict, and error states with associated errors,
focus management, status announcements, and no forced loss of entered values.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Test editable-field and verified-attribute transition rules.
- Integration/contract: Verify ownership, recent-auth, validation, and projection updates.
- Component: Test form and verification states.
- Journey/live: Correct representative data and confirm its permitted public/private projections.
- Manual/rendered: Verify accessible error recovery and sensitive-data masking.

## L2-PRIV-006: Export personal data securely

**Traces to:** L1-PRIV-002

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

An Account holder can request a documented, machine-readable export of their personal data. Export
generation is asynchronous, audited, rate limited, and delivered through an expiring, revocable
mechanism after recent authentication.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given an authenticated Account passes recent authentication, when an export is requested, then one idempotent Job is created and its progress is visible without blocking the request.
2. Given an export completes, when its manifest is inspected, then it identifies formats and categories while excluding other users' private data, secrets, internal moderator notes, and unsafe object references.
3. Given an export link is expired, revoked, reused contrary to policy, or belongs to another Account, when download is attempted, then no export bytes or existence details are disclosed.
4. Given export generation fails, when retry bounds are exhausted, then the requester receives a safe failure state and an operator can diagnose it without reading export content.

### States and accessibility

The flow exposes queued, processing, ready, expired, failed, rate-limited, and empty states with
announced progress and keyboard-accessible download and revoke actions.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Test request idempotency, expiry, revocation, and manifest filtering.
- Integration/contract: Verify Job, authorization, object storage, audit, and download behavior.
- Component: Test every export lifecycle state.
- Journey/live: Request, download, revoke, expire, and cross-Account probe an export.
- Manual/rendered: Review format documentation and assistive-technology announcements.

## L2-PRIV-007: Deactivate and restore an Account

**Traces to:** L1-PRIV-002

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Deactivation is the canonical reversible `active → deactivated` Account transition and remains
distinct from `deletion-pending → deleted`. It first resolves every registered responsibility, then
revokes ordinary Sessions, prevents participation and optional Deliveries, hides the Account as
policy requires, and preserves a time-bounded restoration path unless a Moderation Action forbids it.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given recent authentication, acknowledged consequences, and no unresolved owner/controller/billing/organizer/integration responsibility, when an Account holder deactivates, then lifecycle becomes `deactivated`, ordinary Sessions are revoked, and protected participation stops atomically.
2. Given a deactivated Account is within the restoration window and not prohibited, when ownership is re-established through a restricted-remediation Session and current responsibilities, eligibility, and independent Actions pass, then the Account returns to the documented state without silently restoring banned Memberships.
3. Given a deactivated Account receives background or realtime work, when the worker reauthorizes it, then non-mandatory delivery is suppressed.

### States and accessibility

Destructive confirmation names reversible consequences and restoration limits, supports cancellation,
returns focus safely, and does not rely on color or motion.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Test deactivate/restore transitions and moderation interaction.
- Integration/contract: Verify Session revocation, participation gates, and delivery suppression.
- Component: Test confirmation, completed, restoration, forbidden, and expired states.
- Journey/live: Deactivate from one device and verify all active devices and pending work.
- Manual/rendered: Review destructive language and keyboard flow.

## L2-PRIV-008: Complete an Account deletion request

**Traces to:** L1-PRIV-002

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Account deletion is the canonical `active|deactivated → deletion-pending → deleted` workflow with
recent authentication or restricted remediation, a disclosed cooling-off period, all dependent
responsibility preconditions, legal exceptions, progress state, cancellation rules, and a durable
completion record that contains no unnecessary personal data.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given an Account is a required Community/Space owner or manager, billing contact, Event organizer, Conversation controller, invitation/request owner, Webhook Endpoint owner, owes an unresolved billing action, or is subject to an authorized hold, when deletion is requested, then the server explains the transfer/cancel/close/retention precondition and does not enter `deletion-pending`.
2. Given all preconditions and recent ordinary or restricted-remediation authentication are satisfied, when deletion is confirmed, then Sessions are revoked and one idempotent deletion workflow is scheduled for the end of any cooling-off period.
3. Given the requester proves control through a restricted-remediation Session and cancels before the disclosed irreversible boundary, when cancellation commits, then destructive Jobs cannot erase the Account and no ordinary Session is granted until current lifecycle/eligibility policy permits it.
4. Given the workflow completes, when the former credentials or export links are used, then access fails and the completion record contains only the data required to prove processing.
5. Given cancellation, a new or expanded legal hold, or another lifecycle version change races any destructive batch, when that batch claims work and reaches each irreversible database, blob, projection, or provider boundary, then the worker and hold/cancellation contend on the same monotonic scope epoch: an earlier hold invalidates and defers the worker, while an earlier conditional `irreversible-start` marker lets a later hold report that exact unavailable portion and protect the remainder; stale work aborts, and already erased data is never represented as preserved.

### States and accessibility

The flow exposes consequences, blockers, scheduled date, cancellation, in-progress, exception, and
completed states using accessible confirmation and non-coercive copy.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Test preconditions, cooling-off, cancellation, hold fence, and terminal transitions.
- Integration/contract: Race cancellation and hold creation with database, blob, projection, provider, and credential deletion.
- Component: Test blockers and all lifecycle states.
- Journey/live: Complete and cancel deletion in isolated full-stack data sets.
- Manual/rendered: Review disclosure accuracy and destructive-action accessibility.

## L2-PRIV-009: Enforce retention schedules

**Traces to:** L1-PRIV-003

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Every retained category has a configured start event, duration or review condition, terminal action,
and accountable owner. A durable Job applies schedules and exposes safe metrics for overdue records
without placing personal values in telemetry.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given data reaches its retention deadline without a valid hold, when retention processing runs, then the configured deletion or anonymization action occurs idempotently.
2. Given a valid hold covers the record, when retention processing runs, then the terminal action is deferred and the restricted hold reference is audited.
3. Given retention processing falls behind its service objective, when the threshold is crossed, then an owned alert identifies category and age without disclosing subject data.
4. Given a retention lease or batch races hold creation, expansion, release, or lifecycle cancellation, when it reaches an irreversible boundary, then its versioned deletion authorization conditionally records `irreversible-start` against the same scope epoch changed by the competing transition; exactly the earlier commit wins, later work reloads the resulting hold or loss state, and stale work cannot delete, resurrect, or claim completion.

### States and accessibility

Authorized dashboards distinguish healthy, due, overdue, held, failed, and unknown states in text and
support keyboard and screen-reader inspection.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Test deadline, hold, and terminal-action policies with a deterministic clock.
- Integration/contract: Verify scheduler, leases, hold-version races, irreversible-boundary checks, deletion, and audit behavior.
- Component: Test aggregate operator states without personal data.
- Journey/live: Age representative records and observe processing and alerting.
- Manual/rendered: Review schedule ownership, dashboard clarity, and privacy of metrics.

## L2-PRIV-010: Propagate deletion to derived systems

**Traces to:** L1-PRIV-003

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Deletion and visibility-changing events propagate within documented bounds to relational replicas,
Attachments, Search, caches, Feed projections, analytics, queued work, Notifications, Deliveries, and
integration destinations. Backup copies expire through the declared backup lifecycle.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given an eligible record is deleted, when the propagation objective elapses, then it cannot be retrieved through any active product read, object URL, index, cache, export, or queued Delivery.
2. Given one derived destination is unavailable, when propagation fails, then a retryable tracked state remains and the deletion is not falsely marked complete.
3. Given an encrypted backup still contains the record, when its retention expires or it is restored for recovery, then expiry or replayed deletion prevents the record from reappearing in service.
4. Given an authorized operator inspects deletion progress, when a destination is pending, then the operator sees destination type and status without unnecessary subject content.

### States and accessibility

Subject-facing progress is understandable without exposing topology; operator views announce partial,
failed, retried, and complete states and do not encode status by color alone.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Test destination-state aggregation and completion invariants.
- Integration/contract: Verify every configured store, queue, cache, Search, and media adapter.
- Component: Test safe subject and operator progress views.
- Journey/live: Seed data across all active destinations, delete it, restore a backup, and prove no resurrection.
- Manual/rendered: Review propagation inventory and evidence against the data map.

## L2-PRIV-012: Restrict legal holds and safety evidence

**Traces to:** L1-PRIV-003

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

A legal hold or safety-evidence exception has a defined authority, narrow scope, reason, start,
review or expiry, and restricted access policy. It preserves only the required material and never
silently restores retained data to ordinary product views.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given an authorized role creates a hold, when it commits, then scope, authority, reason code, reviewer, start, review date, and Audit Event are recorded.
2. Given a held subject requests export or deletion, when the request is processed, then disclosable data follows policy while restricted evidence remains hidden and the exception is communicated appropriately.
3. Given a hold expires or is released, when processing runs, then deferred retention actions resume and access grants are removed.
4. Given an unauthorized operator requests held evidence, when authorization runs, then no content or existence detail is disclosed and the attempt is audited.
5. Given an authorized hold is created or its scope expands while deletion or retirement work exists, when it contends on the shared scope epoch, then a hold that commits before `irreversible-start` invalidates matching destructive leases before success is acknowledged, while a hold that loses to an earlier irreversible marker reports the exact already-unavailable portion and protects all remaining scope; workers recheck the resulting fence and no loss is concealed.

### States and accessibility

Restricted tools visibly identify elevated context, expiry, and scope; require explicit confirmation;
and support accessible tables, dialogs, and error recovery.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Test hold scope/version, expiry, release, destructive fences, and retention interaction.
- Integration/contract: Race hold creation/expansion with destructive workers and verify evidence access and audit.
- Component: Test restricted, expiring, released, and forbidden views.
- Journey/live: Apply, inspect, expire, and release a hold through authorized and unauthorized actors.
- Manual/rendered: Complete privacy/security review of permissions and disclosures.

## L2-PRIV-013: Keep telemetry free of sensitive content

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Logs, traces, metrics, diagnostics, support artifacts, and error responses exclude credentials,

2. Given an unexpected exception occurs, when the API returns Problem Details, then the response contains a stable code and correlation identifier without stack, secret, or private payload data.
3. Given a telemetry field is not on the declared schema, when validation or CI checks run, then release is blocked or the field is dropped according to policy.

### States and accessibility

Operator diagnostics present correlation and remediation text without requiring access to private
payloads; error states and filters remain keyboard and screen-reader accessible.

### Verification

- Integration/contract: Inject representative secrets and sensitive bodies through failures and inspect sinks.

## L2-PRIV-016: Resolve dependent responsibilities before Account exit

**Traces to:** L1-PRIV-002

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

One Account-responsibility registry is authoritative for voluntary exit and forced eligibility-loss
continuity. It
includes Community and Space continuity, billing contacts and obligations, Event organization,
Conversation control, owned Moderation Cases, Appeals, and Support Cases, pending Membership
invitations/requests, Webhook Endpoints and credentials, guardian authority and consents, owned
exports, active legal holds, and any capability that declares a last-controller invariant.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given an Account requests deactivation/deletion or is about to lose participation eligibility, when preflight runs, then every registered capability returns a current typed `resolved`, `transfer`, `cancel`, `close`, `restricted-stewardship`, `retain-under-hold`, or `blocked` result with no unregistered responsibility silently ignored.
2. Given a transferable responsibility has an eligible successor, when the successor accepts and exit commits, then transfer becomes authoritative before the Account loses ordinary access and no resource becomes ownerless.
3. Given a responsibility cannot be transferred, when the configured cancel, close, or requeue path is confirmed, then pending invitations/requests, Event/RSVP reminders, Conversation control, case assignments, guardian consent, Webhook Endpoints/credentials, and external Deliveries stop or move to their declared terminal state before exit.
4. Given preflight state changes concurrently, when exit commits, then versions are rechecked atomically and a stale result returns conflict without partial lifecycle or responsibility changes.
5. Given a legal hold applies, when exit proceeds as allowed, then ordinary access may end while held evidence remains restricted; deletion cannot claim completion until the declared exception and later lifecycle are recorded.

### States and accessibility

Preflight groups responsibilities by transfer, cancel, close, hold, and resolved status; it names
successor acceptance and consequences, supports keyboard completion, and preserves focus on conflict.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Test registry completeness, result types, transfer ordering, hold, and concurrency rules.
- Integration/contract: Exercise every capability precondition, successor acceptance, terminal work, and stale race.
- Component: Test preflight summary, remediation links, confirmation, conflict, and completion.
- Journey/live: Exit an Account that owns one instance of every supported dependent responsibility.
- Manual/rendered: Privacy, billing, governance, safety, operations, and accessibility review.

## L2-PRIV-017: Govern guardian consent and eligibility changes

**Traces to:** L1-PRIV-001

**Status:** proposed

**Source:** product brief + research

**Related:** —

Where a reviewed market policy requires guardian authority, the platform defines proof method,
relationship scope, permitted decisions, evidence minimization, expiry/review, withdrawal, disputed
authority, age-out, and the Account holder's independent privacy rights. Eligibility is current policy
state, not a one-time onboarding flag, and every affected surface re-evaluates it.

### Acceptance criteria

1. Given guardian authority is required, when consent is accepted, then verified authority, subject Account, consent scope, policy version, decision time, expiry/review, and minimum protected evidence reference are recorded without retaining raw proof beyond the reviewed need.
2. Given consent is withdrawn, expires, is disputed, or a policy/market decision makes the Account ineligible, when reevaluation commits, then participation restrictions apply immediately and the responsibility registry atomically transfers, cancels, retires, or places bounded stewardship over any last-owner, billing-contact, organizer, Conversation-controller, Endpoint-owner, or similar duty before ordinary authority is lost; all affected Memberships, Messages, Events/RSVPs, checkout, Feeds/Search/public reads, exports, Jobs, Deliveries, caches, realtime, and integrations apply the current eligibility matrix.
3. Given eligibility is later restored or the Account ages out of guardian control, when the transition commits, then only restrictions owned by eligibility are removed, guardian authority and unnecessary evidence expire, and independent Blocks or Moderation Actions remain.
4. Given an Account holder requests access, correction, export, deactivation, deletion, support, or Appeal, when guardian policy applies, then the reviewed subject-versus-guardian authority matrix governs each action and never removes the subject's non-delegable privacy and safety rights.
5. Given eligibility changes after an RSVP, waitlist place, scheduled Delivery, Public Projection, or external event exists, when reconciliation runs, then requests and queued work reauthorize before their next effect, derived capacity and projections converge within the accepted eligibility-propagation objective, default five minutes, and a privacy-minimal notice is available through an allowed channel.

### States and accessibility

Guardian pending, verified, expiring, withdrawn, disputed, age-out, restricted, and restored states use
age-appropriate plain language, separate subject/guardian controls, and accessible localized notices.

### Verification

- Domain/unit: Test authority scope, evidence record, expiry, withdrawal, dispute, age-out, and rights matrix.
- Integration/contract: Reevaluate every named request, read, projection, Job, Delivery, billing, Event, and external boundary.
- Component: Test subject/guardian flows and restricted/restored states.
- Journey/live: Complete consent, withdrawal, policy change, age-out, export, and deletion journeys.
- Manual/rendered: Child-safety, privacy/legal, security, localization, and accessibility review without compliance claims.
